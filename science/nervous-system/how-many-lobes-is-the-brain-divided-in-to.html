How many lobes is the brain divided in to?

(A) 1

(B) ** 4

(C) 10

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Traditionally, each of the hemispheres has been divided into four lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal and occipital.

Concept note-2: -There are four lobes in the cortex, the frontal lobe, parietal lobe, temporal lobe, occipital lobe.

Concept note-3: -Each cerebral hemisphere divides into four separate lobes by a central sulcus, parieto-occipital sulcus, and lateral fissure. The central sulcus runs posterior-medial to anterior-lateral and separates the frontal lobe from the parietal lobe.

Concept note-4: -Each brain hemisphere (parts of the cerebrum) has four sections, called lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal and occipital.

Concept note-5: -Each cerebral hemisphere is divided into five lobes, four of which have the same name as the bone over them: the frontal lobe, the parietal lobe, the occipital lobe, and the temporal lobe. A fifth lobe, the insula or Island of Reil, lies deep within the lateral sulcus.
