Which system do we associate with the sentence “Fight or flight”?

(A) CNS

(B) ANS (Autonomic)

(C) PNS

(D) ** SNS (Sympathetic)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your sympathetic nervous system is a network of nerves that helps your body activate its “fight-or-flight” response. This system’s activity increases when you’re stressed, in danger or physically active.

Concept note-2: -The sympathetic nervous system functions like a gas pedal in a car. It triggers the fight-or-flight response, providing the body with a burst of energy so that it can respond to perceived dangers. The parasympathetic nervous system acts like a brake.

Concept note-3: -The parasympathetic nervous system predominates in quiet “rest and digest” conditions while the sympathetic nervous system drives the “fight or flight” response in stressful situations. The main purpose of the PNS is to conserve energy to be used later and to regulate bodily functions like digestion and urination.

Concept note-4: -Our sympathetic nervous system is important because sometimes we need our bodies to have a rapid, involuntary response, especially in situations of danger. That’s why our SNS is often referred to as our ‘fight or flight’ nervous system.
