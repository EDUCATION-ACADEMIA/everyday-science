Organs, such as the stomach and the small and large intestines, are lined with involuntary muscle tissue. These organs, due to the muscular contractions of their walls, involuntarily ____

(A) Add liquids to food to make it a slurry.

(B) ** Move food through the digestive tract.

(C) Secrete mucous.

(D) Supply digestive enzymes that aid in digestion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Peristalsis is a series of wave-like muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract. It starts in the esophagus where strong wave-like motions of the smooth muscle move balls of swallowed food to the stomach.

Concept note-2: -Peristalsis is a type of involuntary muscle movement that occurs in your digestive system. It begins in your throat when you swallow, and continues to propel food and fluids throughout your gastrointestinal tract.

Concept note-3: -Peristalsis happens in our throats after we initially swallow our food. The food is moved down the esophagus by this involuntary muscle movement and delivered to the stomach. The muscles that preform peristalsis contract and relax in a way that is radially symmetrical.

Concept note-4: -Smooth muscle is present throughout the body, where it serves a variety of functions. It is in the stomach and intestines, where it helps with digestion and nutrient collection.
