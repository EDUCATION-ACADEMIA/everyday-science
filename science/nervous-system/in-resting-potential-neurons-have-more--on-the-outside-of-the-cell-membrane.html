In resting potential, neurons have more ____ on the outside of the cell membrane.

(A) K+

(B) NaCl

(C) ** Na+

(D) Cl-

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In neurons, K+ ions are maintained at higher concentrations within the cell; however, outside the cell, Na+ ions are present at higher-level concentrations. The cell possesses potassium and sodium leakage channels that allow the two cations to release their concentration gradient.

Concept note-2: -The resting membrane potential of a neuron is about-70 mV (mV=millivolt)-this means that the inside of the neuron is 70 mV less than the outside. At rest, there are relatively more sodium ions outside the neuron and more potassium ions inside that neuron.

Concept note-3: -Cl-is more concentrated outside than inside the cell. Na+ is more concentrated outside than inside the cell.

Concept note-4: -At rest, the membrane is impermeable to Na+, as all of the Na+ channels are closed.
