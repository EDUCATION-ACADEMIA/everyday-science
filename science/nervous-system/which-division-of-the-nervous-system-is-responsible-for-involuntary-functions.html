Which division of the nervous system is responsible for involuntary functions?

(A) Parasympathetic

(B) Sympathetic

(C) Somatic

(D) ** Autonomic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The autonomic nervous system is a component of the peripheral nervous system that regulates involuntary physiologic processes including heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, digestion, and sexual arousal.

Concept note-2: -Although most of the autonomic nervous system responses are involuntary, they can integrate with the somatic nervous system, which is responsible for the voluntary movements. For example, in the case of defecation, there is an interplay between voluntary and involuntary movements.

Concept note-3: -The part of your brain that runs autonomic functions is your hypothalamus.
