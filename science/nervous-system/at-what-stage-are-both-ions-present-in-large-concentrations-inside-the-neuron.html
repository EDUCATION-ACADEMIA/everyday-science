At what stage are both ions present in large concentrations INSIDE the neuron?

(A) ** Depolarizarion

(B) Repolarization

(C) Hyperpolarization

(D) Resting Potential

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Depolarization is caused when positively charged sodium ions rush into a neuron with the opening of voltage-gated sodium channels.

Concept note-2: -During the depolarization phase, the gated sodium ion channels on the neuronâ€™s membrane suddenly open and allow sodium ions (Na+) present outside the membrane to rush into the cell. As the sodium ions quickly enter the cell, the internal charge of the nerve changes from-70 mV to-55 mV.

Concept note-3: -There is a higher concentration of potassium ions on the inside of the cell than on the outside.

Concept note-4: -In neurons, potassium ions are maintained at high concentrations within the cell while sodium ions are maintained at high concentrations outside of the cell. The cell possesses potassium and sodium leakage channels that allow the two cations to diffuse down their concentration gradient.
