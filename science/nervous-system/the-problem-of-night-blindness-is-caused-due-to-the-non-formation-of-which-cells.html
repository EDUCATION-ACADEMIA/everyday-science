The problem of night blindness is caused due to the non formation of which cells

(A) Cones

(B) ** Rods

(C) Iodopsin

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rods have a singular photopigment, rhodopsin. Retinol is a vitamin A-derived cofactor that is required for the formation of rhodopsin; thus, VAD leads to impairment of rod function and causes nyctalopia, or night blindness due to the eyeâ€™s inability to adapt from light to dark.

Concept note-2: -Rods are a type of photoreceptor cell present in the retina that transmits low-light vision and is most responsible for the neural transmission of nighttime sight.

Concept note-3: -Vitamin A deficiency, which causes reduced photosensitivity of rhodopsin (a chromoprotein) in rod cells, causes night blindness that is usually not severe, and vision most often recovers when adequate levels of the vitamin are administered.

Concept note-4: -Possible Causes Cataracts. Retinitis pigmentosa. Vitamin A deficiency, especially in individuals who have undergone intestinal bypass surgery. Diabetes.

Concept note-5: -Rhodopsin serves as a visual purple pigment of rod cells and is responsible for night vision. Rhodopsin deficiency causes night blindness.
