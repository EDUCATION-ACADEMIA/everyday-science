EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is the Central Nervous System? The central nervous system is made up of the brain and spinal cord: The brain controls how we think, learn, move, and feel. The spinal cord carries messages back and forth between the brain and the nerves that run throughout the body.

Concept note-2: -The central nervous system is the bodyâ€™s processing centre. The brain controls most of the functions of the body, including awareness, movement, thinking, speech, and the 5 senses of seeing, hearing, feeling, tasting and smelling.

Concept note-3: -The nervous system takes in information through our senses, processes the information and triggers reactions, such as making your muscles move or causing you to feel pain. For example, if you touch a hot plate, you reflexively pull back your hand and your nerves simultaneously send pain signals to your brain.

Concept note-4: -Together, the brain and spinal cord form the central nervous system.

Concept note-5: -The brain controls all the activities in the body. It is the command centre for memory, thinking and all the voluntary, as well as involuntary processes in the body. It also processes the sensory signal from the eyes, ears, nose and skin. The spinal cord act as a pathway between the brain and peripheral nervous system.
