Which of the following are the blood vessels that have a wall that is 1 cell thick?

(A) Veins

(B) Arteries

(C) ** Cappillaries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: One-cell thick vessels are called capillaries.

Concept note-2: -Capillaries. Found near every living cell of the body. Microscopic – walls are one cell thick, this allows for the diffusion of substances into the cells from the capillaries and out of the cells into the capillaries.

Concept note-3: -Capillaries connect the smallest branches of arteries and veins. The capillaries are where molecules are exchanged between the blood and the body’s cells. The walls of capillaries are just one cell thick. Capillaries therefore allow molecules to diffuse across the capillary walls.

Concept note-4: -Answer. Capillaries are vessels that are one cell thick and are responsible for exchange of nutrients and waste between the blood and cells.

Concept note-5: -Capillaries. (A) Electron micrograph of a cross section of a small capillary in the pancreas. The wall is formed by a single endothelial cell surrounded by a basal lamina.
