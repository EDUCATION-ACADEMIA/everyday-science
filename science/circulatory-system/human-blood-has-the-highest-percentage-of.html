Human blood has the highest percentage of.

(A) Eosinophils

(B) Basophils

(C) ** Neutrophils

(D) Monocytes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Neutrophils: 40% to 60% Lymphocytes: 20% to 40% Monocytes: 2% to 8% Eosinophils: 1% to 4%

Concept note-2: -The normal range of neutrophils in a healthy adult is between 2, 500 and 7, 000 neutrophils per microliter of blood. Any number above 7, 000 or below 2, 500 puts you at risk of a neutrophil condition.

Concept note-3: -Introduction. Neutrophils, also known as polymorphonuclear (PMN) leukocytes, are the most abundant cell type in human blood. They are produced in the bone marrow in large numbers, Â 1011 cell per day.

Concept note-4: -In terms of percentage proportion, the neutrophils are found in higher proportion (60-65 percent) when compared to the other WBCs. This is followed by the lymphocytes, which are about 20-25 percent. Monocytes contribute to 6-8 percent of the leucocytes.

Concept note-5: -Absolute neutrophil count For example, a 70% Relative Neutrophil Count may seem within normal limits. However, if the total WBC is 30, 000, the absolute value (70% x 30, 000) of 21, 000 would be an abnormally high count. A normal Neutrophils Count is between 2, 500 and 7, 000.
