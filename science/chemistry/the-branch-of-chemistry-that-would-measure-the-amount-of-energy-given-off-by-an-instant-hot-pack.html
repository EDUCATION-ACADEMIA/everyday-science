The branch of chemistry that would measure the amount of energy given off by an instant hot pack.

(A) ** Physical Chemistry

(B) Organic Chemistry

(C) Biochemistry

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Calorimetry is used to measure amounts of heat transferred to or from a substance.

Concept note-2: -Calorimetry is the process of measuring the amount of heat released or absorbed during a chemical reaction.

Concept note-3: -Chemical thermodynamics deals with the relationship between heat and other forms of chemical energy, kinetics with chemical reaction rates. Subdisciplines of physical chemistry include electrochemistry, photochemistry (see photochemical reaction), surface chemistry, and catalysis.

Concept note-4: -Thermochemistry is a branch of chemistry that qualitatively and quantitatively describes the energy changes that occur during chemical reactions. Energy is the capacity to do work. The first law of thermodynamics states that the energy of the universe is constant.

Concept note-5: -Thermochemistry is the study of the heat energy which is associated with chemical reactions and/or phase changes such as melting and boiling. A reaction may release or absorb energy, and a phase change may do the same.
