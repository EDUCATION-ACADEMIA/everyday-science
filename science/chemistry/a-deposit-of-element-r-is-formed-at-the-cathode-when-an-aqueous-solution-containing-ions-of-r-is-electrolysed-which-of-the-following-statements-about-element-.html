A deposit of element R is formed at the cathode when an aqueous solution containing ions of R is electrolysed. Which of the following statements about element R is correct?

(A) R forms a negative ion

(B) ** R ions gain electrons at the cathode

(C) R ions donate electrons at the cathode

(D) R is above hydrogen in the electrochemical series

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -the metal is produced at the cathode if it is less reactive than hydrogen. hydrogen is produced at the cathode if the metal is more reactive than hydrogen.

Concept note-2: -A solid deposit of element R is formed at the cathode when an aqueous solution containing ions of R is electrolysed.

Concept note-3: -Electrolysis of aqueous solutions is a process in which an electrical current is passed through an aqueous (water-based) solution to cause chemical reactions. This process splits the ions in the solution into individual atoms, allowing them to gain or lose electrons.

Concept note-4: -If an aqueous solution of NaCl is electrolysed, the product obtained at the cathode is H2 gas and not Na .
