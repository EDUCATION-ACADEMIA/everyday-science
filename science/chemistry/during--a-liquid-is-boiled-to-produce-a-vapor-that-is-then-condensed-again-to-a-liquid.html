During ____, a liquid is boiled to produce a vapor that is then condensed again to a liquid.

(A) ** Distillation

(B) Filtration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Distillation is the process that occurs when a liquid sample is volatilized to produce a vapor that is subsequently condensed to a liquid richer in the more volatile components of the original sample.

Concept note-2: -Distillation is the process of vaporizing a liquid and recovering it by condensing the vapors. The liquid formed by this condensation is called the distillate.

Concept note-3: -distillation, process involving the conversion of a liquid into vapour that is subsequently condensed back to liquid form.

Concept note-4: -DISTILLATION IS A PROCESS that relies on evaporation to purify water. Contaminated water is heated to form steam. Inorganic compounds and large non-volatile organic molecules do not evaporate with the water and are left behind. The steam then cools and condenses to form purified water.
