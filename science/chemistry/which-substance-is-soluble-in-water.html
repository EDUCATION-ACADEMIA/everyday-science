Which substance is soluble in water?

(A) sand

(B) gravel

(C) ** salt

(D) wood shavings

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The two substances which are soluble in water are salt and sugar as they get completely dissolved in it.

Concept note-2: -Common salt is the only material that is soluble in water because it disappears completely when dissolved in water.

Concept note-3: -Some common examples of soluble salts include sodium chloride, potassium nitrate, and calcium chloride.

Concept note-4: -The most common soluble salts present in materials used for ceramics are: Calcium, magnesium sodium, potassium and aluminum sulphates.

Concept note-5: -Substances to try to dissolve, e.g. sugar, coffee, pepper, sand, flour, salt.
