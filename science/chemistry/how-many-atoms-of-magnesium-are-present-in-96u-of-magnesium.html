How many atoms of magnesium are present in 96u of Magnesium

(A) 0.25

(B) 25

(C) ** 4

(D) 0.4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -We know that 1 mole of any substance contains 6.023 into 10 to the power 23 number of atoms or molecules. Hence, in 4 moles of magnesium we will have 2.4092 into 10 to the power 24 atoms of magnesium, so the number of magnesium atoms present in 96 unit masses 2.4092, any 4.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: There are 2.409 X 1024 atoms of magnesium in a sample of 4 moles of magnesium. Using Avogadro’s number, we know that there are 6.022 X 1023 atoms in every mole of our sample.

Concept note-3: -Number of atoms of Mg = Moles×Avogadro number = 2×6. 023×1023=12.

Concept note-4: -Hence the Magnesium will contain 24.08 × 10²³ atoms.
