Calculating and finding out how much nitrogen is in a sample of air is which study

(A) organic

(B) inorganic

(C) ** analytical

(D) physical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Finding out how much nitrogen is in a sample of air is:Organic Chemistry.

Concept note-2: -Composition of Air Air is made up of 78.09% nitrogen, 20.95% oxygen, 0.93% argon, 0.04% carbon dioxide, and other gases in meagre amounts.

Concept note-3: -Air is nothing but a mixture of a variety of gasses. The air in the atmosphere consists of nitrogen, oxygen, which is the life-sustaining substance for animals and humans, carbon dioxide, water vapour and small amounts of other elements (argon, neon, etc.).

Concept note-4: -Answer: Air is composed of 78 percent nitrogen. Oxygen accounts for 21 percent, and the inert noble gas argon makes up 0.9 percent of the air.

Concept note-5: -Sometimes, the word “atmosphere” is used instead of the word “air.” Standard Dry Air is the composition of gases that make up air at sea level. It is a standard scientific unit of measurement. Standard Dry Air is made up of nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, neon, helium, krypton, hydrogen, and xenon.
