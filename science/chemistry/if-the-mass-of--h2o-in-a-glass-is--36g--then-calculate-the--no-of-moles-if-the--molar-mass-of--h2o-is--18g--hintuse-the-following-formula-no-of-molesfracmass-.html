If the mass of H 2 O in a glass is 36 g . then calculate the no . of moles if the molar mass of H 2 O is 18 g . HINT:use the following formula: No . of moles = Mass in g Molar mass

(A) ** 2 moles

(B) 0.5 moles

(C) 5 moles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Complete step by step answer: We know that the molecular mass of water is $18g/mol$. One can calculate the number of moles in a substance using the above formula. Hence, the number of moles in $36g$ of water is two moles.

Concept note-2: -Therefore, in one mole of water (H2O), there are two moles of hydrogen atoms and one mole of oxygen atoms.

Concept note-3: -Hence the number of moles of 32 grams of water is 1.77 moles.
