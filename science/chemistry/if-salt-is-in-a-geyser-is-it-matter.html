if salt is in a geyser is it matter

(A) ** NO!

(B) YES

(C) maybe

(D) semi-matter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Salty water or excessive chlorine content in water causes deterioration of the surface coating which can lead to tank corrosion and tank leakage. This can severely affect the lifespan of your geyser.

Concept note-2: -Let the heater dry completely and then slightly expand the coil by pulling from both ends. This should break the salt layer which is not as flexible as the metal. When the deposits need to be removed again, restore the coil to the original shape.

Concept note-3: -When there’s no hot water, the problem can stem from lack of power, a faulty electric thermostat or a faulty upper electric heating element. Start by ruling out power problems. First, reset any tripped circuit breaker, and replace any blown fuse.
