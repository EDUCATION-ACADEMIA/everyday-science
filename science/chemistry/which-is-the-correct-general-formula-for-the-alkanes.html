Which is the correct general formula for the alkanes?

(A) ** CnH2n+2

(B) CnH2n

(C) CnH2n+1OH

(D) CnH2n+1COOH

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, the general formula C n H 2 n + 2 represents alkanes.

Concept note-2: -Alkanes have the general formula CnH2n+2 and can be subdivided into the following three groups: the linear straight-chain alkanes, branched alkanes, and cycloalkanes. Alkanes are also saturated hydrocarbons.

Concept note-3: -Alkanes are a series of compounds that contain carbon and hydrogen atoms with single covalent bonds. These are known as saturated hydrocarbons. This group of compounds consists of carbon and hydrogen atoms with single covalent bonds. Also comprises a homologous series having a molecular formula of CnH2n+2.

Concept note-4: -The general formula for alkanes is CnH2n+2, where n stands for number of carbon atoms and 2n+2 for number of hydrogen atoms in the molecule.In alkanes, tetrahedra are joined together in which C-C and C-H bond lengths are 154 pm and 112 pm respectively (Unit 12).
