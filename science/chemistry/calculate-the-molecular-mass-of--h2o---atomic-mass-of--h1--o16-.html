Calculate the molecular mass of H 2 O . ( Atomic mass of H = 1 , O = 16 )

(A) 17

(B) ** 18

(C) 14

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Using the periodic table of the elements to find atomic weights, we find that hydrogen has an atomic weight of 1, and oxygen’s is 16. In order to calculate the molecular weight of one water molecule, we add the contributions from each atom; that is, 2(1) + 1(16) = 18 grams/mole.

Concept note-2: -The molecular weight of hydrogen peroxide is 34 .

Concept note-3: -Molecular formula of water is H2O. Atomic mass of H=1 u and atomic mass of O=16 u. Thus, molecular mass of H2O=2×1+16=18 u.

Concept note-4: -Hydrogen has an atomic mass of 1 and oxygen atomic mass of 16, so H2O has a mass of 16+2×1=18 g/mol.

Concept note-5: -Molar mass of H2O=2×1+16=18g/mol.
