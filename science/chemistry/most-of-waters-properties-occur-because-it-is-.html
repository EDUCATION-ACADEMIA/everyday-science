Most of water’s properties occur because it is ____

(A) ** A polar covalent

(B) Made with oxygen

(C) A polar ionic

(D) Makes up 75% of the Earth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom within water molecules (H2O) form polar covalent bonds. While there is no net charge to a water molecule, the polarity of water creates a slightly positive charge on hydrogen and a slightly negative charge on oxygen, contributing to water’s properties of attraction.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: Two properties of water that result from the polar bonding between its molecules are cohesion and adhesion. These properties cause water molecules to ‘stick’ to each other and to be attracted to other polar molecules.

Concept note-3: -Summary. Water molecules are polar, so they form hydrogen bonds. This gives water unique properties, such as a relatively high boiling point, high specific heat, cohesion, adhesion and density.

Concept note-4: -Water molecules attract each other due to polarity. Polarity: Although the net charge of a water molecule is zero, water is polar because of its shape. The hydrogen ends of the molecule are positive and the oxygen end is negative. This causes water molecules to attract each other and other polar molecules.

Concept note-5: -Water is a Polar Covalent Molecule The unequal sharing of electrons between the atoms and the unsymmetrical shape of the molecule means that a water molecule has two poles-a positive charge on the hydrogen pole (side) and a negative charge on the oxygen pole (side).
