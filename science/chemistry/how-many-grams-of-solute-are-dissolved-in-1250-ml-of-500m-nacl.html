How many grams of solute are dissolved in 125.0 mL of 5.00M NaCl?

(A) 0.625 g NaCl

(B) 36.52 g NaCl

(C) ** 36.5 g NaCl

(D) 0.625 mol NaCl

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount of solute will dissolve in 125mL of the solution-36.53g. Solution: To find the amount of solute in 125 mL of NaCl we need to find the amount of solute the will dissolve in 1 liter. = 36.53g.

Concept note-2: -grams of solute = (wt% solution) x (ml of water) ÷ (100 – wt% solution)

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: 12% NaCl solution means 12g of NaCl is dissolved in 100 mL solution.

Concept note-4: -What is the molarity of a solution which contains 58.5 g of sodium chloride dissolved in . 500 L of solution? Molarity=moles/volume, 58.5 g of NaCl equals 1 mole, so Molarity=1/. 5, which is 2.

Concept note-5: -We multiply 2.5 g NaCl by the conversion factor of 1 mole NaCl over the formula weight of NaCl, 58.5 g. That tells us that we have 0.0427 mole of NaCl. Now that we know the moles we can calculate the molarity. Moles of solute (0.0427) divided by the volume of the solution (0.125 L) gives us 0.34 M NaCl.
