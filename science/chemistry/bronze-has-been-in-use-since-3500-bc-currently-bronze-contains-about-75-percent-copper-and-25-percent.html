Bronze has been in use since 3500 B.C Currently bronze contains about 75 percent copper and 25 percent.

(A) Zinc

(B) ** Tin

(C) Led

(D) Iron

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -bronze, alloy traditionally composed of copper and tin. Modern bronze is typically 88 percent copper and about 12 percent tin.

Concept note-2: -There are many different bronze alloys, but typically modern bronze is 88% copper and 12% tin.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: When copper and tin are mixed, they form the alloy bronze. Bronze consists of about 90% copper and 10% tin.

Concept note-4: -Bronze is an alloy typically composed of 90 percent copper and 10 percent tin, and, because it has a lower melting point than pure copper, it will stay liquid longer when filling a mold. It also produces a better casting than pure copper and has superior tensile strength.
