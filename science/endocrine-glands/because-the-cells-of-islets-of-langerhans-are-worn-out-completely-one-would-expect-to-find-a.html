Because the cells of Islets of Langerhans are worn out completely, one would expect to find a.

(A) Low blood glucose level

(B) Normal blood glucoses level but high urine glucose level

(C) High blood glucose level but low urine glucose level

(D) ** High blood glucose level and high urine glucose level

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Islets of Langerhans are responsible for regulating blood glucose by releasing insulin hormone. If these cells stop functioning, then blood glucose will rise which will lead to Diabetes.

Concept note-2: -The alpha cells of the islets of Langerhans produce an opposing hormone, glucagon, which releases glucose from the liver and fatty acids from fat tissue.

Concept note-3: -A pancreatic cell that produces hormones (e.g., insulin and glucagon) that are secreted into the bloodstream. These hormones help control the level of glucose (sugar) in the blood. Also called endocrine pancreas cell and islet cell.

Concept note-4: -The islet of Langerhans regulates glucose homeostasis through dose-dependent secretion of hormones including insulin from the -cells and glucagon from -cells1.
