This substance plays a role in regulating circadian rhythms (sleep patterns), and is produced by the pineal body.

(A) Benadryl

(B) ** Melatonin

(C) Melanin

(D) Ambien

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Melatonin is the only known hormone synthesized by the pineal gland and is released in response to darkness hence the name, “hormone of darkness” [10]. Melatonin provides a circadian and seasonal signal to the organisms in vertebrates.

Concept note-2: -Melatonin is a hormone that’s mainly produced by your pineal gland. The importance of pineal melatonin in humans is not clear, but many researchers believe it may help to synchronize circadian rhythms in different parts of your body.

Concept note-3: -In vertebrate animals, including humans, the master clock is a group of about 20, 000 nerve cells (neurons) that form a structure called the suprachiasmatic nucleus, or SCN. The SCN is in a part of the brain called the hypothalamus and receives direct input from the eyes.

Concept note-4: -The main function of the pineal gland is to receive information about the state of the light-dark cycle from the environment and convey this information by the production and secretion of the hormone melatonin.

Concept note-5: -Circadian rhythm sleep disorders Melatonin serves as a time cue (signal of darkness) to various organs including the SCN itself and in the absence of light, may entrain the sleep–wake and neuroendocrine rhythms to the 24 h cycle (reviewed in Zisapel, 2001).
