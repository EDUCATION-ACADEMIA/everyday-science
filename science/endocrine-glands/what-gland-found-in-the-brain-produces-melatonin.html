What gland found in the brain produces melatonin?

(A) ** Pineal

(B) Pituitary

(C) Thymus

(D) Thyroid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Melatonin is a hormone secreted by the enigmatic pineal gland in response to darkness, hence the name hormone of darkness.

Concept note-2: -The pineal gland produces melatonin, which helps maintain circadian rhythm and regulate reproductive hormones.

Concept note-3: -The pineal gland is located deep in the brain in an area called the epithalamus, where the two halves of the brain join. In humans, this is situated in the middle of the brain; it sits in a groove just above the thalamus, which is an area that co-ordinates a variety of functions related to our senses.

Concept note-4: -The pineal gland secretes melatonin, which is a hormone that helps regulate circadian rhythms. Melatonin is produced according to the amount of light a person is exposed to. The pineal gland releases greater amounts of melatonin when it is dark, which points to melatoninâ€™s role in sleep.

Concept note-5: -The production and release of melatonin from the pineal gland occurs with a clear daily (circadian) rhythm, with peak levels occurring at night. Once produced, it is secreted into the blood stream and cerebrospinal fluid (the fluid around the brain & spinal cord) and conveys signals to distant organs.
