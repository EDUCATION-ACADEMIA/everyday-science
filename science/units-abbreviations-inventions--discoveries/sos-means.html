SOS means.

(A) Sample Of Statues

(B) Series Of Schools

(C) Save Our State

(D) ** Save Our Souls

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -SOS stands for Save Our Souls. It is also referred as Save Our Ship. It is a Morse code which is used as a distress code to signal danger. The code signals that a person is in danger and needs immediate help. The SOS distress code is a sequence of three dots, three dashes and three dots without any space.

Concept note-2: -SOS, when it was first agreed upon by the International Radio Telegraphic Convention in 1906, was merely a distinctive Morse code sequence and was initially not an abbreviation. Later in popular usage it became associated with mnemonic phrases such as “Save Our Souls” and “Save Our Ship".

Concept note-3: -What does SOS stand for? It’s not “Save Our Ship” or “Save Our Souls” like you might think. Instead, the distress signal that originated in the maritime community is communication used specifically for ships, based on Morse Code, that is represented by three dots, three dashes, and three more dots.

Concept note-4: -The expression “Save Our Ship” was probably coined by sailors to signal for help from a vessel in distress.

Concept note-5: -To ensure that one’s soul will have a pleasant experience in the afterlife, as in heaven, instead of experiencing damnation.
