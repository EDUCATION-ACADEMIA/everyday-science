What is a scientific law?

(A) an explanation of hypotheses, results, and observations

(B) the final answer to a scientific question

(C) a scientific statement

(D) ** an explanation of many experimental results and observations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is a Law? Scientific laws are similar to scientific theories in that they are principles that can be used to predict the behavior of the natural world. Both scientific laws and scientific theories are typically well-supported by observations and/or experimental evidence.

Concept note-2: -Natural law is another name for scientific law. Scientists use the scientific method to test hypotheses.

Concept note-3: -In general, a scientific law is the description of an observed phenomenon. It doesnâ€™t explain why the phenomenon exists or what causes it. The explanation for a phenomenon is called a scientific theory. It is a misconception that theories turn into laws with enough research.

Concept note-4: -In science, the word theory means a comprehensive explanation of an important feature of nature that is supported by many facts gathered over time.

Concept note-5: -An explanation of things or events that is based on knowledge gained from many observations and experiments is a theory.
