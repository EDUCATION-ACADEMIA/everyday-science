The process by which metamorphic rocks are formed ____

(A) ** heat and pressure

(B) erosion and deposition

(C) melting and cooling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -New minerals are created either by rearrangement of mineral components or by reactions with fluids that enter the rocks. Pressure or temperature can even change previously metamorphosed rocks into new types. Metamorphic rocks are often squished, smeared out, and folded.

Concept note-2: -If there is too much heat or pressure, the rock will melt and become magma. This will result in the formation of an igneous rock, not a metamorphic rock. Consider how granite changes form. Granite is an igneous rock that forms when magma cools relatively slowly underground.

Concept note-3: -Hydrothermal Metamorphism Rocks that are altered at high temperatures and moderate pressures by hydrothermal fluids are hydrothermally metamorphosed.

Concept note-4: -Metamorphic rocks arise from the transformation of existing rock to new types of rock in a process called metamorphism.

Concept note-5: -Metamorphism is the addition of heat and/or pressure to existing rocks, which causes them to change physically and/or chemically to become a new rock. Metamorphic rocks may change so much that they resemble the original rock. Any type of rock – igneous, sedimentary, or metamorphic – can become a metamorphic rock.
