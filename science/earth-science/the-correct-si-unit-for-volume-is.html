The correct SI unit for volume is

(A) ** centimeters cubed (cm3) or milliliters (mL)

(B) kelvin

(C) gram

(D) square meter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -milliliter is a unit of measurement that is used to measure the volume in the SI unit. A milliliter is equivalent to one cubic Centimeter or 1 / 1, 000, 000 cubic metres and 1 / 1000 litres. The abbreviation used for milliliters is ‘ML’.

Concept note-2: -The SI unit of volume is the cubic meter (m3), which is a derived unit. Liter (L) is a special name for the cubic decimeter (dm3).

Concept note-3: -A cubic centimetre (or cubic centimeter in US English) (SI unit symbol: cm3; non-SI abbreviations: cc and ccm) is a commonly used unit of volume that corresponds to the volume of a cube that measures 1 cm × 1 cm × 1 cm. One cubic centimetre corresponds to a volume of one millilitre.

Concept note-4: -SI Unit of Volume In addition to this, standard metric prefixes are used to highlight large or small volume measures. Often, millilitre (ml) is also used as a unit for measuring volume.

Concept note-5: -A cubic centimeter (cm3 or cc) is a measurement of volume. It is a derived unit of the International System of Units (SI). 1 cubic centimeter is equal to the volume of a cube with edges that measure 1 cm each. Since the volume of a cube is calculated as length × width × height, the volume of the cube is 1 cm3.
