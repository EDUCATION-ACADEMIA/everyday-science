Rust that forms on rocks or metal because of extended exposure to oxygen is what example of chemical weathering?

(A) carbonation

(B) acid rain

(C) ** oxidation

(D) living organisms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oxidation. The most common example of oxidation is when rust occurs on iron. Oxidation occurs when oxygen reacts with metal elements in rock or on other surfaces creating oxides.

Concept note-2: -Another type of chemical weathering works on rocks that contain iron. These rocks turn to rust in a process called oxidation. Rust is a compound created by the interaction of oxygen and iron in the presence of water. As rust expands, it weakens rock and helps break it apart.

Concept note-3: -Rust, for example, is iron oxide. When rocks, particularly those with iron in them, are exposed to air and water, the iron undergoes oxidation, which can weaken the rocks and make them crumble.

Concept note-4: -Rust that forms on rocks or metal because of extended exposure to oxygen is what example of chemical weathering.

Concept note-5: -Oxidation is a type of chemical weathering, that is why it causes things to weather or wear away.
