How does the moon get its light?

(A) ** it reflects the sun’s light onto the earth

(B) it creates its own light, similar to a star

(C) it gathers light from earth during the day

(D) the moon is actually a yellow color, so it appears to be bright during the nighttime

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Unlike a lamp or our sun, the moon doesn’t produce its own light. Moonlight is actually sunlight that shines on the moon and bounces off. The light reflects off old volcanoes, craters, and lava flows on the moon’s surface.

Concept note-2: -Moonlight is actually a reflected light as the moon is a natural satellite that is not a source of light. It reflects all the light from the sun.

Concept note-3: -Moonlight is really light from the sun. The moon reflects the sunlight. When something is reflected, it is thrown back. The sun’s light is thrown back to Earth.

Concept note-4: -The Sun produces light by a nuclear reaction called fusion. As atoms of hydrogen combine to form helium, they produce vast amounts of heat and light.

Concept note-5: -The moon is not creating the light and heat, but is reflecting energy from the sun and sending it 220, 000 miles across space to add a romantic glow to the Earth’s night.
