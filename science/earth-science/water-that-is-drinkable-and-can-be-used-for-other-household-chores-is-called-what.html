Water that is drinkable and can be used for other household chores is called what?

(A) Vitamin water

(B) Good water

(C) Safe water

(D) ** Potable water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Potable water, also known as drinking water, comes from surface and ground sources and is treated to levels that that meet state and federal standards for consumption.

Concept note-2: -Why is it Called Potable Water? Potable comes from the Latin potare, meaning “to drink.” The Romans came up with the word and built some of the world’s first aqueducts, above-ground channels that brought potable water from the mountains to the cities.

Concept note-3: -What do we mean by ‘non-potable water’? Non-potable water is not suitable for drinking but may still be used for other purposes. Potable water is water of a quality suitable for drinking, cooking and personal bathing.

Concept note-4: -Examples of potable drinking water facilities include: pipe-borne water, protected well, borehole, protected spring water and rainwater.

Concept note-5: -Potable water is the water which is filtered and treated properly and is finally free from all contaminants and harmful bacteria. This purified water is fit to drink, or it can be called ‘drinking water after the purification processes and is safe for both cooking and drinking.
