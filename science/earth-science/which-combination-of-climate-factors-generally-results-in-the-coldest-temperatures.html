Which combination of climate factors generally results in the coldest temperatures?

(A) low elevation and low latitude

(B) low elevation and high latitude

(C) high elevation and low latitude

(D) ** high elevation and high latitude

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Expert-Verified Answer. The combination of high elevation and high latitudes generally results in the coldest temperatures. Latitude is defined as the distance from the equator in regard to the North and South poles.

Concept note-2: -At higher latitudes, the angle of solar radiation is smaller, causing energy to be spread over a larger area of the surface and cooler temperatures.

Concept note-3: -The two most important factors in the climate of an area are temperature and precipitation. The yearly average temperature of the area is obviously important, but the yearly range in temperature is also important. Some areas have a much larger range between highest and lowest temperature than other areas.
