This area of science creates and uses maps of physical features of the Earth.

(A) Geology

(B) Geography

(C) ** Cartography

(D) Mapology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Geography is a systematic study of the Earth, its features, and phenomena that take place on it. For something to fall into the domain of geography, it generally needs some sort of spatial component that can be placed on a map, such as coordinates, place names, or addresses.

Concept note-2: -cartography, the art and science of graphically representing a geographical area, usually on a flat surface such as a map or chart. It may involve the superimposition of political, cultural, or other nongeographical divisions onto the representation of a geographical area.

Concept note-3: -Physical geography is the study of Earthâ€™s seasons, climate, atmosphere, soil, streams, landforms, and oceans.

Concept note-4: -Cartography is the science of making maps, which are systematic and symbolic representations of space, usually in two dimensions, and often employing sophisticated mathematical techniques.

Concept note-5: -map, graphic representation, drawn to scale and usually on a flat surface, of features-for example, geographical, geological, or geopolitical-of an area of the Earth or of any other celestial body. Globes are maps represented on the surface of a sphere. Cartography is the art and science of making maps and charts.
