During the month of January, at which locationin New York State is the Sun lowest in the sky atsolar noon?

(A) ** Massena

(B) Niagara Falls

(C) Utica

(D) New York City

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Of those four locations, Massena is the one that’s farthest north, that is, farthest from the equator. lowest in the sky at Massena.

Concept note-2: -The winter solstice is around December 21, marking the date on which the Sun is lowest in the sky at noon and rises and sets farthest south.

Concept note-3: -The opposite occurs at about December 21, the first day of winter (Winter Solstice). The Sun is directly overhead at the Tropic of Capricorn (23.5° S latitude).

Concept note-4: -New York is generally located between 41deg N and 45 deg N latitude, so it never receives the direct rays of the Sun. In other words, the Sun will never be found directly overhead (zenith) at solar noon during any point of the year for a location in NYS.

Concept note-5: -By the June solstice (usually June 21), the sun rises considerably north of due east and sets considerably north of due west.
