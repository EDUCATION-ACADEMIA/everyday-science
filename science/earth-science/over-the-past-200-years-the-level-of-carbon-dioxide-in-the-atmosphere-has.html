Over the past 200 years, the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has

(A) decreased slightly

(B) increased until recently, and then decreased

(C) ** increased faster than in the past

(D) stayed about the same

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbon dioxide concentrations have increased substantially since the beginning of the industrial era, rising from an annual average of 280 ppm in the late 1700s to 414 ppm in 2021 (average of five sites in Figure 1)-a 48 percent increase. Almost all of this increase is due to human activities.

Concept note-2: -Human activities have raised the atmosphere’s carbon dioxide content by 50% in less than 200 years.

Concept note-3: -Since 1970, CO2 emissions have increased by about 90%, with emissions from fossil fuel combustion and industrial processes contributing about 78% of the total greenhouse gas emissions increase from 1970 to 2011. Agriculture, deforestation, and other land-use changes have been the second-largest contributors.

Concept note-4: -Human Activity Is the Cause of Increased Greenhouse Gas Concentrations. Over the last century, burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2). This increase happens because the coal or oil burning process combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2.

Concept note-5: -The concentration of carbon dioxide in Earth’s atmosphere is currently at nearly 412 parts per million (ppm) and rising. This represents a 47 percent increase since the beginning of the Industrial Age, when the concentration was near 280 ppm, and an 11 percent increase since 2000, when it was near 370 ppm.
