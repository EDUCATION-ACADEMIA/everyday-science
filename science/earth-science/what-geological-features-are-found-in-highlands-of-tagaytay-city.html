What geological features are found in highlands of Tagaytay City?

(A) plains

(B) rivers

(C) hills

(D) ** volcano

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Facts about the Taal Volcano: One of the smallest volcanoes in the world. A second most active volcano in the Philippines. Recorded at least 38 eruptions within 450 years. The Taal caldera is filled with lakes because of the past eruptions between 140, 000 and 5, 380 BP.

Concept note-2: -The geological and physiographic features around Taal Lake indicate that the lake basin was formed by faulting, tectonic uplift, and catastrophic volcanism.

Concept note-3: -Taal Volcano is a complex stratovolcano in the province of Batangas, Philippines, on an island in Taal Lake.

Concept note-4: -The city overlooks Manila Bay to its north, Laguna de Bay to its east, and Taal Volcano and Taal Lake to its south. Tagaytay City is both upland hilly and upland mountainous, particularly on its easter and southern sections. Forests, open grasslands, and pine trees cover the said areas.
