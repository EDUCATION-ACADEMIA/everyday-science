What kind of oceans generate hurricanes?

(A) ** warm oceans

(B) cold oceans

(C) icy oceans

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hurricanes form over tropical oceans, where warm water and air interact to create these storms.

Concept note-2: -The Pacific Ocean generates the greatest number of tropical storms and cyclones. The most powerful storms, sometimes called super typhoons, occur in the western Pacific. The Indian Ocean is second in the total number of storms, and the Atlantic Ocean ranks third.

Concept note-3: -Hurricanes form from a cluster of thunderstorms that suck up the warm, moist air and move it high into Earth’s atmosphere. The warm air is then converted into energy that powers the hurricane’s circular winds. These winds spin around a low-pressure center called the eye.

Concept note-4: -China. The fact that China’s typhoon season lasts the entire year makes it the country that experiences the most hurricanes.

Concept note-5: -Tropical cyclone. Polar cyclone. Mesocyclone. Extratropical cyclone.
