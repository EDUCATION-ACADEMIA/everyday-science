What type of plate boundary created the Himalaya mountains?

(A) Continent-Continent Divergent

(B) Continent-Ocean Convergent

(C) Ocean-Ocean Convergent

(D) ** Continent-Continent Convergent

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Himalayan Mountains formed at a convergence plate boundary between the Eurasian Plate and the Indian plate .

Concept note-2: -Himalayan Mountains are an example of a Continent-Continent convergent plate boundary where two slabs of continental crust have collided and compressing the crust and the acretionary wedge material and lifting it to form the Himalayan Mountains.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The Himalayan Mountains formed at a convergence plate boundary between the Eurasian plate and the Indian plate.
