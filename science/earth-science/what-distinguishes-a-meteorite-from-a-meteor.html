What distinguishes a meteorite from a meteor?

(A) ** A meteorite lands on Earth’s surface.

(B) A meteorite flashes in the sky and disappears.

(C) A meteorite stays in space.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Meteor: If a meteoroid enters the Earth’s atmosphere and vaporizes, it becomes a meteor, which is often called a shooting star. Meteorite: If a small asteroid or large meteoroid survives its fiery passage through the Earth’s atmosphere and lands on Earth’s surface, it is then called a meteorite.

Concept note-2: -When meteoroids enter Earth’s atmosphere (or that of another planet, like Mars) at high speed and burn up, the fireballs or “shooting stars” are called meteors. When a meteoroid survives a trip through the atmosphere and hits the ground, it’s called a meteorite.

Concept note-3: -Meteors do not land on the surface of the earth. Meteorites land on the surface of the earth. Meteoroids break down in the earth’s atmosphere, which results in the flash of light known as meteors. Meteorites are the broken meteoroids that land on the earth.

Concept note-4: -Meteoroids become meteors when they crash into Earth’s atmosphere and the gases surrounding them briefly light up as “shooting stars.” While most meteors burn up and disintegrate in the atmosphere, many of these space rocks reach Earth’s surface in the form of meteorites.

Concept note-5: -meteor: A meteoroid that enters Earth’s atmosphere and burns up. meteorite: A meteoroid, especially one that has hit Earth’s surface. asteroid: A rocky object that orbits the sun and has an average size between a meteoroid and a planet.
