rock once melted, cooled and hardened

(A) metamorphic rock

(B) sedimentary rock

(C) fossils

(D) ** igneous rock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Extrusive, or volcanic, igneous rocks are formed when molten hot material cools and solidifies. There are three main types of rocks: sedimentary, igneous, and metamorphic. Each of these rocks are formed by physical changes-such as melting, cooling, eroding, compacting, or deforming-that are part of the rock cycle .

Concept note-2: -Igneous rocks (from the Latin word for fire) form when hot, molten rock crystallizes and solidifies. The melt originates deep within the Earth near active plate boundaries or hot spots, then rises toward the surface.

Concept note-3: -Any rock that forms from the cooling of magma is an igneous rock. Magma that cools quickly forms one kind of igneous rock, and magma that cools slowly forms another kind. When magma rises from deep within the earth and explodes out of a volcano, it is called lava, and it cools quickly on the surface.

Concept note-4: -Igneous rock can change into sedimentary rock or into metamorphic rock.
