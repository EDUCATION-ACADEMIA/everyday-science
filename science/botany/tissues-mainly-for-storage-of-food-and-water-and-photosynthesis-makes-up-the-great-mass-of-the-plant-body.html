Tissues mainly for storage of food and water and photosynthesis; makes up the great mass of the plant body.

(A) Wood Tissues

(B) Apical Tissues

(C) ** Fundamental Tissues

(D) Lateral Tissues

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The functions of parenchyma tissues are storage, photosynthesis, and to help the plant float on water. Collenchyma-Are similar to parenchyma cells with thicker cell walls. They are meant to provide mechanical support to the plant structure in parts such as petiole of the leaf.

Concept note-2: -Parenchyma forms the major component within organs. The parenchymaâ€™s cells are often isodiametric. They have thin cellulose walls. They perform various functions such as Photosynthesis, secretion, and storage.

Concept note-3: -Parenchyma tissues are found in the soft parts of the plant like the leaf. They help in photosynthesis and storage of food. Parenchyma tissues are made of cells with simple structures.

Concept note-4: -Parenchyma cells are responsible for storage of food and water. These cells are living and vacuolated protoplasm. The parenchyma cells of epidermis have cuticles in their walls.

Concept note-5: -Parenchyma cells are generally large. They have large central vacuoles, which allow the cells to store and regulate ions, waste products, and water. Tissue specialised for food storage is commonly formed of parenchyma cells.
