The Salt March was also called  ____ because all the people were joining the procession wearing white Khadi.

(A) ** White flowing river

(B) White revolution

(C) White era

(D) Salt river

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The March was called the ‘white flowing river’ because of the thousands of people marching in white khadi clothes. They reached Dandi, a coastal town by the Arabian Sea, on April 5. They had walked 240 miles in all!

Concept note-2: -Gandhi during the salt march, March 1930. In 1930, Gandhi famously led a march to the sea to collect salt (which Indians were banned from producing), forcing the British Raj into a classic decision dilemma and paving the way for Indian independence.

Concept note-3: -Salt March, also called Dandi March or Salt Satyagraha, major nonviolent protest action in India led by Mohandas (Mahatma) Gandhi in March–April 1930.

Concept note-4: -They were protesting the British monopoly on salt production in India, which caused high prices and poor-quality salt for Indian consumers. The Salt March was one of Gandhi’s most famous protests, and it helped galvanise support for independence from British rule.

Concept note-5: -Salt was chosen to symbolize the start of civil disobedience movement because salt was deemed as something on which each Indian had the basic right. Mahatma Gandhi declared resistance to British salt policies to be the unifying theme for the civil disobedience movement and thus started Dandi March.
