[SOURCES]
Sugar and the products of photosynthesis move through the plant in

(A) ** the phloem cells

(B) the xylem cells

(C) the root hair cells

(D) palisade mesophyll layer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sugars produced in sources, such as leaves, need to be delivered to growing parts of the plant via the phloem in a process called translocation, or movement of sugar. The points of sugar delivery, such as roots, young shoots, and developing seeds, are called sinks.

Concept note-2: -Phloem is a vascular tissue helps in the transportation of food. The end product of photosynthesis is glucose [C6H12O6] synthesized in leaves and transported to the various storage organs like flowers, fruits, seeds and tubers etc.

Concept note-3: -From what you know about osmosis, explain why water follows the sugar as it moves through the Phloem. Water molecules always move to areas of high solute concentration via osmotic pressure.

Concept note-4: -Photosynthesis occurs in the mesophyll cells of leaves. From the leaves, phloem tissue transports it to all other parts of the plant. Phloem tissue is made up of tubular interconnected cells.

Concept note-5: -It is important to the plant that the phloem moves both up and down because the movement of food and nutrients created during photosynthesis throughout the plant is accomplished by the phloem.
