Structures of the root that protect the root from injuries as it grows deeper into the soil are called ____

(A) root feathers

(B) ** root caps

(C) root hairs

(D) root fingers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -covered by a structure called the root cap. The root cap, which contains dead cells, protects the root from injury from rocks and other material as the root grows through the soil. The root cap also makes a slimy substance called mucigel (MUCE-i-jell).

Concept note-2: -Statement 1: Root cap protects the root meristem from the friction of the soil and its outer cells are continuously replaced by newer ones.

Concept note-3: -The root cap, a small tissue at the tip of the root, protects the root from environmental stress and functions in gravity perception. To perform its functions, the position and size of the root cap remains stable throughout root growth.

Concept note-4: -The tip of the root is protected by the root cap, a structure exclusive to roots and unlike any other plant structure. The root cap is continuously replaced because it gets damaged easily as the root pushes through soil.

Concept note-5: -The root cap (RC) is a multilayered dome of spindle-shaped parenchyma cells that overlies the growing root tip. It is present in the roots of almost all crop species.
