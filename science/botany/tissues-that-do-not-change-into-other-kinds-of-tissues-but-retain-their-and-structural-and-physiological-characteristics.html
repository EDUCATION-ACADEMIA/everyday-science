Tissues that do not change into other kinds of tissues but retain their and structural and physiological characteristics

(A) Temporary Tissues

(B) Vascular Tissues

(C) ** Permanent Tissues

(D) Forever Tissues

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simple Permanent Tissue These are also known as homogenous tissues. They are made up of a single cell type, usually with the same origin, structure, and function.

Concept note-2: -Answer: Permanent tissues are made of meristematic cells, that has definite form and shape and have lost the power to divide and differentiate and are of three types-simple, complex and special. The simple tissues are parenchyma, sclerenchyma and collenchyma. Chlorenchyma is a parenchyma, having chloroplast.

Concept note-3: -Permanent tissue is derived from the division of meristematic cells. The cells do not divide because they become differentiated. They may be alive or dead. These cells have different shapes related to the function they perform.

Concept note-4: -The three types of simple permanent tissues are Parenchyma, Collenchyma, and Sclerenchyma.
