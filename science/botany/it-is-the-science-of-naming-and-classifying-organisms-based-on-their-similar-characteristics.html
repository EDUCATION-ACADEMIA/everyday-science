It is the science of naming and classifying organisms based on their similar characteristics.

(A) Identification

(B) Classification

(C) ** Taxonomy

(D) Nomenclature

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Taxonomy is the science of naming, describing and classifying organisms and includes all plants, animals and microorganisms of the world.

Concept note-2: -Taxonomy is the study of relationships between living things and the formal classification of organisms into groups based upon those hypothesized relationships. Organisms are classified based upon their similarities and differences.

Concept note-3: -The term “taxonomy” was developed from two Greek words, “taxis, ” meaning arrangement, and “nomia, ” meaning distribution or method. In simple words, the definition of taxonomy is a branch of science that deals primarily with the description, identification, nomenclature, and classification of organisms.

Concept note-4: -Taxonomy and Nomenclature. Taxonomy and Nomenclature. Taxonomy (sometimes called “systematics") is the science of classifying organisms. The Linnean system of classification, used for both plants and animals, was developed more than two centuries ago by the great Swedish botanist Carolus Linnaeus (born Carl von Linné).

Concept note-5: -There are seven main taxonomic ranks: kingdom, phylum or division, class, order, family, genus, and species.
