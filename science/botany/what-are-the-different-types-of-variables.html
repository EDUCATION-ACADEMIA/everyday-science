What are the different types of variables

(A) ** Quantitative

(B) ** Qualitative

(C) Formulative

(D) Single

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Categorical variables. A categorical variable (also called qualitative variable) refers to a characteristic that can’t be quantifiable. Nominal variables. Ordinal variables. Numeric variables. Continuous variables. Discrete variables. 02-Sept-2021

Concept note-2: -There are three types of categorical variables: binary, nominal, and ordinal variables.

Concept note-3: -There are two general types of variables: qualitative and quantitative. Qualitative variables can be further broken down into nominal, ordinal, and binary, while quantitative variables can be broken down into discrete and continuous.

Concept note-4: -You can see that one way to look at variables is to divide them into four different categories ( nominal, ordinal, interval and ratio).

Concept note-5: -Types of Quantitative Variables Let’s consider different features of variables used in quantitative research studies. Here we explore quantitative variables as being categorical, ordinal, or interval in nature. These features have implications for both measurement and data analysis.
