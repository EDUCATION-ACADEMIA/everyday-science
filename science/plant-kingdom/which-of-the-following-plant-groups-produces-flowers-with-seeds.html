Which of the following plant groups produces flowers with seeds?

(A) ** Angiosperms

(B) Gymnosperms

(C) Pteriophytes

(D) Bryophytes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Angiosperms produce flowers, which are special structures for reproduction. They contain male parts that make pollen and female parts that contain ovules. Some plants have these male and female parts in different flowers.

Concept note-2: -Angiosperms are plants that produce flowers and bear their seeds in fruits. They are the largest and most diverse group within the kingdom Plantae, with about 300, 000 species. Angiosperms represent approximately 80 percent of all known living green plants.

Concept note-3: -Angiosperms, which are flowering plants, are the largest and most diverse group within the kingdom Plantae.

Concept note-4: -Angiosperms are flowering plants. They make up around 80 percent of all the living plant species on Earth.

Concept note-5: -Angiosperms, are also known as flowering plants and having seeds enclosed within their fruit. Whereas gymnosperms have no flowers or fruits and have naked seeds on the surface of their leaves.
