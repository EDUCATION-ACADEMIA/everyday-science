In which two layers of the leaf are most active chloroplasts found?

(A) ** Palisade and spongy mesophyll

(B) Epidermis and stomata

(C) xylem and phloem

(D) Roots and spongy mesophyll

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In leaves, cells in the mesophyll (the tissue between the upper and lower epidermis) are uniquely suited to carry out photosynthesis on a large scale. This is due to their high concentration of chloroplasts, which are the sites of photosynthesis.

Concept note-2: -The mesophyll can be further broken down into two layers, the palisade layer and the spongy layer, both of which are packed with chloroplasts, the factories of photosynthesis.

Concept note-3: -However, the palisade cells usually contain more chloroplasts than the spongy cells.

Concept note-4: -The palisade cells contain a lot of chloroplasts to help them perform this photosynthesis. The palisade cells are closely packed together to maximize light absorption.

Concept note-5: -The palisade mesophyll layer of the leaf is adapted to absorb light efficiently.
