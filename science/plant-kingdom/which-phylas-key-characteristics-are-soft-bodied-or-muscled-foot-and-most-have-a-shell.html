Which phyla’s key characteristics are “soft bodied” or “muscled foot” and most have a shell?

(A) ** Mollusca

(B) Cnidarians

(C) Echinoderms

(D) Arthropods

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The animals belonging to the phylum Mollusca have soft-bodies, triploblastic and bilaterally symmetrical and coelomate.

Concept note-2: -The phylum Mollusca is defined by several special characteristics. These defining characteristics include a mantle with a mantle cavity, a shell (except where lost), visceral mass, foot, and radula. The odontophore is in the mouth of most mollusks and it supports the radula (a ribbon of teeth).

Concept note-3: -mollusk, also spelled mollusc, any soft-bodied invertebrate of the phylum Mollusca, usually wholly or partly enclosed in a calcium carbonate shell secreted by a soft mantle covering the body.

Concept note-4: -Soft-bodied invertebrates, mollusks include slugs, snails, octopuses, squid, clams, and mussels. Most mollusks have SHELLS to protect them.

Concept note-5: -Mollusca: Mollusca is the second largest phylum of invertebrate animals after Arthropoda. Organisms belonging to this phylum have a soft and unsegmented body. The dorsal and ventral sides of the body can be distinguished and are found in marine and freshwater habitats.
