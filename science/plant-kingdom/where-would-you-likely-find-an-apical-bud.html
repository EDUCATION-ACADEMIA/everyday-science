Where would you likely find an apical bud?

(A) ** at the end of a twig.

(B) along the side of a twig.

(C) at the base of a twig

(D) only in the autumn

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An apical bud is the primary growing bud located at the tip of the stem in a plant. Apical buds make auxin that is distributed throughout the plant via the phloem.

Concept note-2: -This tip (apical) end is the region of growth in stem and is called a bud.

Concept note-3: -Terminal buds are located at the apex (tip) of a stem. Lateral (axillary) buds are located on the sides of a stem. They usually arise where a leaf meets a stem (an axil).

Concept note-4: -Types of buds for location: terminal, when located at the tip of a stem (apical is equivalent but rather reserved for the one at the top of the plant); axillary, when located in the axil of a leaf (lateral is the equivalent but some adventitious buds may be lateral too);
