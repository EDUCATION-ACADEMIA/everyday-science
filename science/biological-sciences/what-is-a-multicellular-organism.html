What is a multicellular organism?

(A) an organism with only one cell

(B) bacteria

(C) ** an organism with more than one type of cell

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Multicellular organisms are made of more than one cell and are complex organisms. They are visible to the naked eye. They possess distinct organs and organ systems. They are eukaryotes, i.e., they contain membrane-bound structures.

Concept note-2: -Organisms that are made up of more than one cell are known as multicellular. Multicellular organisms are said to be complex. As they are made up of many different cells. An example of a multicellular organism would be human beings.

Concept note-3: -Multicellular organisms are composed of more than one cell, with groups of cells differentiating to take on specialized functions. In humans, cells differentiate early in development to become nerve cells, skin cells, muscle cells, blood cells, and other types of cells.

Concept note-4: -The Characteristics of the Multicellular Organisms are as Follows: These are complex organisms that are made up of more than one cell. These organisms are visible to the naked eye. They are made up of different organs and the organ systems where each of these performs various functions.
