Are the following listed in the correct order from least complex to most complex? Organism → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biome → Biosphere

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Within the discipline of ecology, researchers work at five broad levels, sometimes discretely and sometimes with overlap: organism, population, community, ecosystem, and biosphere. Let’s take a look at each level.

Concept note-2: -Ecosystems are organized to better understand the frame of reference in which they are being studied. They are organized from smallest to largest; organism, population, community, ecosystem.

Concept note-3: -Levels of organization in ecology include the organism, population, community, ecosystem, biome, and biosphere.

Concept note-4: -Six general levels of the organization listed from smallest to largest are chemical, cellular, tissue, organ, organ system, and organism levels. Chemicals are regarded to be the smallest and lowest unit of organization in a living system, ranging from the smallest atoms to the largest macromolecules.
