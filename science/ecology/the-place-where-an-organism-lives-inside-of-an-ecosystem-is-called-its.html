The place where an organism lives inside of an ecosystem is called its

(A) Biome

(B) Community

(C) ** Habitat

(D) Niche

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A habitat is a place where an organism makes its home. A habitat meets all the environmental conditions an organism needs to survive. For an animal, that means everything it needs to find and gather food, select a mate, and successfully reproduce.

Concept note-2: -A place where an organism or a community of organisms live, including the living and nonliving conditions of their surrounding environment, is called a habitat. The habitats of all living organisms make up the Earth’s biosphere.

Concept note-3: -The term “habitat” has several meanings. In ecology it means either the area and resources used by a particular species (the habitat of a species) or an assemblage of animals and plants together with their abiotic environment.

Concept note-4: -Answer: Solution: The surroundings where plants and animals live is called their habitat. For example, the habitat of a frog is fresh water, while the habitat of a camel is a desert.

Concept note-5: -Habitat: The specific physical location where a particular organism lives or is adapted to live in a community.
