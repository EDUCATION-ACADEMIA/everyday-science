How does nitrogen get to the ground from the atmosphere?

(A) ** rain and lightning

(B) thunder

(C) heat

(D) dangifino

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As lightning blasts through the atmosphere, it breaks apart nitrogen molecules. This allows them to combine with oxygen in the air to form nitrogen oxides. The rain dissolves these into nitrates, then carries them to Earth and into the soil.

Concept note-2: -Rain storms contribute atmospheric nitrogen through rain drops that reach the soil. Legumes, such as soybeans, alfalfa and clovers, are plants that can convert atmospheric nitrogen into plant-usable nitrogen.

Concept note-3: -Lightning does add nitrogen to the soil, as nitrates dissolve in precipitation. This helps plants, but microorganisms in the soil do the vast majority of nitrogen fixation.

Concept note-4: -Rainwater contains nitrate-the most bio-available form of nitrogen. Nitrogen is one of the three key macro-nutrients that plants need to thriveâ€“necessary for the development of lush foliage. Many forms of nitrogen are not actually absorbable by plants.
