When the energy flow through an ecosystem is measured at each trophic level, ____ of the available energy passes from one level to the next

(A) ** 10%

(B) 5%

(C) 1%

(D) 50%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount of energy at each trophic level decreases as it moves through an ecosystem. As little as 10 percent of the energy at any trophic level is transferred to the next level; the rest is lost largely through metabolic processes as heat.

Concept note-2: -Energy decreases as it moves up trophic levels because energy is lost as metabolic heat when the organisms from one trophic level are consumed by organisms from the next level.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct answer is ‘Chemical energy’.

Concept note-4: -On average, only about 10% of the energy stored as biomass in one trophic level-e.g., primary producers-gets stored as biomass in the next trophic level-e.g., primary consumers. Put another way, net productivity usually drops by a factor of ten from one trophic level to the next.
