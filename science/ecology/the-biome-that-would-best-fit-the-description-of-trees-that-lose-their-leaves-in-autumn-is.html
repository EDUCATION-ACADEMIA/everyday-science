The biome that would best fit the description of “trees that lose their leaves in autumn” is:

(A) tundra

(B) ** deciduous forest

(C) desert

(D) coniferous forest

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deciduous temperate forests (sometimes called summer-green forests) are dominated by broadleaved trees which lose their leaves during winter.

Concept note-2: -The deciduous forest is characterized by trees that lose their leaves at the end of each growing season.

Concept note-3: -A deciduous forest is a biome dominated by deciduous trees which lose their leaves seasonally. The Earth has temperate deciduous forests, and tropical and subtropical deciduous forests, also known as dry forests. Another name for these forests is broad-leaf forests because of the wide, flat leaves on the trees.

Concept note-4: -deciduous forest, vegetation composed primarily of broad-leaved trees that shed all their leaves during one season.

Concept note-5: -The Temperate Deciduous Forest Temperate deciduous forests can be found in the eastern part of the United States and Canada, most of Europe and parts of China and Japan. The temperate deciduous forest is a biome that is always changing.
