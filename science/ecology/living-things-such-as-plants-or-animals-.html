living things such as plants or animals ____

(A) ** organisms

(B) species

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organisms: All living things in the environment are organisms, such as plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms. Population: A population of organisms is a group of individuals from the same species living in a specific area at the same time.

Concept note-2: -An organism refers to a living thing that has an organized structure, can react to stimuli, reproduce, grow, adapt, and maintain homeostasis. An organism would, therefore, be any animal, plant, fungus, protist, bacterium, or archaeon on earth. These organisms may be classified in various ways.

Concept note-3: -Living things are divided into five kingdoms: animal, plant, fungi, protist and monera. Living things are divided into five kingdoms: animal, plant, fungi, protist and monera. Living things are divided into five kingdoms: animal, plant, fungi, protist and monera.

Concept note-4: -These organisms acting like both plants and animals are called mixotrophs because they mix (combine) different ways of getting nutrition. These fascinating creatures are not rare freaks of nature, but are very common.

Concept note-5: -Cells are made up of components that help living things to eat, respire, excrete wastes, and perform all of the necessary functions of life. The components are organized, which means that they fit and work together. For this reason, living things are called organisms.
