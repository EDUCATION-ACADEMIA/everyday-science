Which of the following describes a food web?

(A) ** complex

(B) dependent on one or two organisms

(C) linear

(D) mostly tertiary consumers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a complex food web, various interaction pathways may connect pairs of species. A high carnivore might prey upon both a lesser carnivore and the prey of the lesser carnivore, forming a “loop” in the food web. This report contains one technical definition for stability of an ecosystem.

Concept note-2: -Which of the following best describes what the food web represents within an ecosystem? Explanation: The food web presents a graphical representation of the interconnected food chains within an ecosystem. In other words, food chains illustrate the trophic levels of a particular ecosystem.

Concept note-3: -Theoretically, compartments increase the stability in networks, such as food webs.” Food webs are also complex in the way that they change in scale, seasonally, and geographically. The components of food webs, including organisms and mineral nutrients, cross the thresholds of ecosystem boundaries.

Concept note-4: -Each living thing is a part of multiple food chains – for example, grass is a part of the food chain: grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → eagle, and also of the food chain: grass → deer → tiger. All of the interconnected and overlapping food chains in a habitat make up a food web.
