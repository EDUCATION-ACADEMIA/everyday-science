Only 10 percent of the energy stored in an organism can be passed on to the next trophic level. Of the remaining energy, some is used for the organism’s life processes, and the rest is

(A) Used in reproduction

(B) Stored as body fat

(C) Stored as body tissue

(D) ** Eliminated as heat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount of energy at each trophic level decreases as it moves through an ecosystem. As little as 10 percent of the energy at any trophic level is transferred to the next level; the rest is lost largely through metabolic processes as heat.

Concept note-2: -The 10% Rule means that when energy is passed in an ecosystem from one trophic level to the next, only ten percent of the energy will be passed on. A trophic level is the position of an organism in a food chain or energy pyramid.

Concept note-3: -Final answer: In an ecosystem, the 10% of energy available for transfer from one trophic level to the next is in the form of chemical energy.

Concept note-4: -10% law. When organisms are consumed, approximately 10% of the energy in the food is fixed into their flesh and is available for next trophic level (carnivores or omnivores). When a carnivore or an omnivore in turn consumes that animal, only about 10% of energy is fixed in its flesh for the higher level.
