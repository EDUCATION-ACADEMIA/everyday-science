This biome is like the rainforest of the sea and has warm shallow waters that are filled with many types of sea creatures

(A) Estuary

(B) ** Coral reef

(C) Intertidal Zone

(D) Open Ocean

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The coral reef is one of the major marine biomes. Although it is a relatively small biome, around 25% of the known marine species live in coral reefs.

Concept note-2: -Coral reefs are some of the most precious habitat in the ocean-which has earned them the nickname “rainforests of the sea.” They’re a complicated ecosystem where thousands of species are supported by some of the smallest of all-corals.

Concept note-3: -They are some of the most biologically-rich ecosystems in the world, supporting thousands of different species of fish, coral, sponges, and more.

Concept note-4: -Coral reefs are located in three primary regions located between 20 degrees N and 20 degrees S of the equator: the Indo-Pacific, the Western Atlantic, and the Red Sea. The Indo-Pacific region stretches from southeast Asia through Polynesia and Australia, eastward across the Indian Ocean to Africa.
