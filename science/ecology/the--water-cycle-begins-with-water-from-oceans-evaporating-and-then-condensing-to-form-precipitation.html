The ____ water cycle begins with water from oceans evaporating and then condensing to form precipitation

(A) ** environmental

(B) recycler

(C) oceanic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When warmed by the sun, water on the surface of oceans and freshwater bodies evaporates, forming a vapor. Water vapor rises into the atmosphere, where it condenses, forming clouds. It then falls back to the ground as precipitation. Moisture can also enter the atmosphere directly from ice or snow.

Concept note-2: -The water cycle shows the continuous movement of water within the Earth and atmosphere. It is a complex system that includes many different processes. Liquid water evaporates into water vapor, condenses to form clouds, and precipitates back to earth in the form of rain and snow.

Concept note-3: -Precipitation, evaporation, freezing and melting and condensation are all part of the hydrological cycle-a never-ending global process of water circulation from clouds to land, to the ocean, and back to the clouds.

Concept note-4: -Water vapour in the air gets cold and changes back into liquid, forming clouds. This is called condensation. Precipitation occurs when so much water has condensed that the air cannot hold it anymore. The clouds get heavy and water falls back to the earth in the form of rain, hail, sleet or snow.

Concept note-5: -Along with evaporation and condensation, precipitation is one of the three major parts of the global water cycle. Precipitation forms in the clouds when water vapor condenses into bigger and bigger droplets of water. When the drops are heavy enough, they fall to the earth.
