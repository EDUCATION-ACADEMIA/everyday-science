Which of these relationships is harmful?

(A) crocodile and plover bird

(B) Two scavenger birds fighting to eat a dead animal

(C) ** A flea on a dog

(D) A ladybug eating aphids on a plant

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A parasitic relationship is one in which one organism, the parasite, lives off of another organism, the host, harming it and possibly causing death. The parasite lives on or in the body of the host. A few examples of parasites are tapeworms, fleas, and barnacles.

Concept note-2: -The last example of symbiosis is parasitism. This is when one organism benefits but while doing so it is harming the other member in the relationship. A common example of this is a flea on a dog. The flea obtains nutrients and protection from the dog but the dog has an uncomfortable skin reaction because of the flea.

Concept note-3: -The host is the organism that is harmed by the other. Parasites are defined as organisms that live in or on another organism (host) and benefit at the hostâ€™s expense. Tapeworm eggs are carried around by fleas. When fleas bite your cat or dog they can transfer the eggs over to hatch.

Concept note-4: -Out of these, fleas and dogs share a parasitic kind of symbiosis. In this kind of relationship, one organism (in this case, fleas) lives on another (dogs) organism also called host organism, as a parasite. They keep extracting nutrients from the host body that benefits them but weakens the host body.

Concept note-5: -Parasites are often tiny organisms that feed off, and weaken, their hosts. the host unlike tapeworms and ringworms who live inside of you. An example of parasitism is fleas and dogs. The flea attaches to the dog and feeds on its blood.
