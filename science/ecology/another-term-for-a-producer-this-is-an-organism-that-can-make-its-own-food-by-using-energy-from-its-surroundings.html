Another term for a producer. This is an organism that can make its own food by using energy from its surroundings.

(A) Consumer

(B) Omnivore

(C) ** Autotroph

(D) Heterotroph

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An autotroph is an organism that can produce its own food using light, water, carbon dioxide, or other chemicals. Because autotrophs produce their own food, they are sometimes called producers. Plants are the most familiar type of autotroph, but there are many different kinds of autotrophic organisms.

Concept note-2: -An autotroph is an organism that can produce its own food using inorganic substances. In contrast, heterotrophs are organisms that cannot produce their own nutrients and require consumption of other organisms to live.

Concept note-3: -Autotrophs are known as producers because they are able to make their own food from raw materials and energy. Examples include plants, algae, and some types of bacteria. Heterotrophs are known as consumers because they consume producers or other consumers. Dogs, birds, fish, and humans are all examples of heterotrophs.

Concept note-4: -Autotrophs are vital to all ecosystems because all organisms need organic molecules and only autotrophs can produce them from inorganic compounds. There are two basic types of autotrophs: photoautotrophs and chemoautotrophs.

Concept note-5: -Autotrophs are organisms that prepare their own food by trapping light energy and converting them into food. Autotrophs include algae, plants, and some bacteria. For example; plants prepare food by trapping the light from sunlight.
