Which of the following represents the hydrolysis of protein?

(A) ** Polypeptides + water → dipeptides

(B) Polypeptides → dipeptides + water

(C) Dipeptides → polypeptides + water

(D) Dipeptides + water → polypeptides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Protein hydrolysis is the breakdown of protein into smaller peptides and free amino acids. The sequence starts from proteins which is broken down to peptones and then to polypeptides and finally to amino acids.

Concept note-2: -The result is 2 individual amino acid molecules.

Concept note-3: -Dipeptide Structure A dipeptide is formed when two amino acids are joined together by a peptide bond. Amino acids consist of an amino and carboxyl functional groups. An amino group consists of one nitrogen and two hydrogens, bonded together through single covalent bonds.

Concept note-4: -Key Concepts. Proteins are polypeptides, polymers made up of a large number of amino acid units joined together by peptide bonds (amide links). Hydrolysis refers to a reaction with water. Acid hydrolysis refers to a hydrolysis reaction occurring under acidic conditions.
