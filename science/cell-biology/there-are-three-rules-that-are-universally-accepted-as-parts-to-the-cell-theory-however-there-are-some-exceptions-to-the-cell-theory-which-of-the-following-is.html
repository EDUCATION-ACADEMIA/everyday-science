There are three “rules” that are universally accepted as parts to the cell theory. However, there are some exceptions to the cell theory. Which of the following is an exception?

(A) All living things are made up of cells

(B) Cells are the basic structural and functional units of life

(C) Cells come from pre-existing cells

(D) ** Mitochondria and chloroplasts are self-replicating and can reproduce independently

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1) All organisms are made of cells. 2) All existing cells are produced by other living cells. 2) All existing cells are produced by other living cells. 3) The cell is the most basic unit of life.

Concept note-2: -Presence of extra-chromosomal DNA: Mitochondria and chloroplasts are exceptions as they have their own genetic material and can reproduce independently.

Concept note-3: -The correct option is C All cells are surrounded by cell wall. Schleiden, Schwann and Virchow contributed to the formation of cell theory. It states that: All living beings are made up of one or more cells.

Concept note-4: -Which organism is considered an exception to the cell theory because it has a noncellular structure? 33. Viruses are exceptions to the cell theory, but they have some characteristics of living things.
