From smallest to largest, what are the levels of multicellular organization?

(A) Cell, Tissue, Organ System, Organ

(B) Cell, Organ, Tissue, Organ System

(C) Organ System, Organ, Tissue, Cell

(D) ** Cell, Tissue, Organ, Organ System

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The major levels of organization in the body, from the simplest to the most complex are: atoms, molecules, organelles, cells, tissues, organs, organ systems, and the human organism.

Concept note-2: -From simplest to most complex, the proper levels of organization in multicellular organisms are: Page 2 The levels of biological organization in order from smallest to largest are: atom → molecule → macromolecule → organelle → cell → tissue → organ → organ system → whole organism.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The organization of life is ordered as following from the smallest to largest; cells, tissues, organs, organ systems, organism.

Concept note-4: -Biologists recognize 12 levels of organization in the living world. From the simplest to the most complex, these levels include atoms, molecules, organelles, cells, tissues, organs, organ systems, organisms, populations, communities, ecosystems, and finally biospheres.

Concept note-5: -An organism is made up of four levels of organization: cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems. These levels reduce complex anatomical structures into groups; this organization makes the components easier to understand.
