What organelle stores waste and water in both plant and animal cells?

(A) Lysosomes

(B) Golgi Complex

(C) ** Vacuoles

(D) Endoplasmic Reticulum

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vacuoles are membrane-bound organelles that can be found in both animals and plants. In a way, theyâ€™re specialized lysosomes. That is to say that their function is really to handle waste products, and by handle, mean take in waste products and also get rid of waste products.

Concept note-2: -In plant cells, the function of vacuoles is to store water and maintain turgidity of the cell. Vacuoles in animal cells store water, ions and waste.

Concept note-3: -Both plant and animal cells have vacuoles. A plant cell contains a large, singular vacuole that is used for storage and maintaining the shape of the cell. In contrast, animal cells have many, smaller vacuoles.

Concept note-4: -A vacuole stores salts, minerals, pigments and proteins within the cell. The solution that fills a vacuole is known as the cell sap. The vacuole is also filled with protons from the cytosol that helps in maintaining an acidic environment within the cell. A large number of lipids are also stored within the vacuoles.

Concept note-5: -Vacuoles in plant cells are large because their main function is to store water for the plant. The central vacuole in a plant cell can take up to 30 to 90 percent of the area of the cell.
