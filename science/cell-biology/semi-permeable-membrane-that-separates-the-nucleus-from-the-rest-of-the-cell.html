Semi-permeable membrane that separates the nucleus from the rest of the cell

(A) cell wall

(B) chromosomes

(C) ** nuclear membrane

(D) nucleolus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nuclear envelope separates the contents of the nucleus from the cytoplasm and provides the structural framework of the nucleus.

Concept note-2: -Nuclear Membrane The nuclear membrane is a double layer that encloses the cell’s nucleus, where the chromosomes reside. The nuclear membrane serves to separate the chromosomes from the cell’s cytoplasm and other contents.

Concept note-3: -The nuclear membrane, also called the nuclear envelope, is a double membrane layer that separates the contents of the nucleus from the rest of the cell. It is connected to the endoplasmic reticulum.

Concept note-4: -Cell Membrane (Plasma Membrane) The cell membrane consists of a lipid bilayer that is semipermeable. The cell membrane regulates the transport of materials entering and exiting the cell. National Human Genome Research Institute.

Concept note-5: -The nuclear membrane is a semi-permeable membrane as the nuclear pores allow only selective molecules to move inside or outside the nucleus.
