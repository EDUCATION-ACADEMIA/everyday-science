If a sequence of genes on a chromosomes is changed from ABCDEFG to GFEDCBA is

(A) Deletion

(B) ** Inversion

(C) Translocation

(D) Addition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Inversions. An inversion occurs when a chromosome breaks in two places; the resulting piece of DNA is reversed and re-inserted into the chromosome. Genetic material may or may not be lost as a result of the chromosome breaks.

Concept note-2: -An inversion mutation is one type of mutation. Inversion mutations occur when a section of DNA breaks away from a chromosome during the reproductive process and then reattaches to the chromosome in reversed order.

Concept note-3: -Inversions are a special type of mutation in which a piece of chromosomal DNA is flipped 180 degrees. For an inversion to occur, two breaks occur in a chromosome, the region between the breaks gets inverted, and the ends of the region get rejoined to the rest of the chromosome.
