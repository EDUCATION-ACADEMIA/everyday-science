What is diffusion?

(A) ** Diffusion is the process by which molecules move from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration.

(B) Diffusion is the diffusion of water molecules through a selectively permeable membrane.

(C) Diffusion is that they “pick up” molecules outside of the cells and carry them in, using energy.

(D) Diffusion is that the cell membane surrounds and engulfs, or encloses, a particle. Once the particles is engulfed, the cell membrane wraps around the particle and forms a vacuole within the cell.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the process of diffusion, a substance tends to move from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration until its concentration becomes equal throughout a space.

Concept note-2: -Final answer: Movement of molecules from their higher concentration to their lower concentration is called diffusion.

Concept note-3: -Osmosis is a specific type of diffusion; it is the passage of water from a region of high water concentration through a semi-permeable membrane to a region of low water concentration.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The process by which molecules spread out and move from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration is called diffusion. Diffusion is considered a passive process compared to an active process.

Concept note-5: -Diffusion is the movement of a substance from an area of high concentration to an area of lower concentration . Diffusion occurs in liquids and gases when their particles collide randomly and spread out. Diffusion is an important process for living things-it is how substances move in and out of cells.
