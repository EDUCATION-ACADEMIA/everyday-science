This organelle is stores food, water and waste.

(A) Ribosome

(B) Cytoplasm

(C) ** Vacuole

(D) Mitochondria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A vacuole is a membrane-bound cell organelle. In animal cells, vacuoles are generally small and help sequester waste products. In plant cells, vacuoles help maintain water balance.

Concept note-2: -The vacuole is a large, round sac found in the cytoplasm. It stores water, food, wastes, or other materials needed by the cell.

Concept note-3: -The vacuole holds large amounts of water or food. Donâ€™t forge that vacuoles can also hold the plant waste products. Those waste products are slowly broken into small pieces that cannot hurt the cell. Vacuoles hold onto things that the cell might need, just like a backpack.

Concept note-4: -A vacuole is an organelle found in plant cells that houses food, water, and waste.

Concept note-5: -Food, water and wastes are stored inside vacuoles in cells. Animal and plant cells contain vacuoles. Plant cell vacuoles are larger than animal cell vacuoles, but animal cells contain more of them.
