A crosome in a sperm is secreted by.

(A) Endoplasmic reticulum

(B) Mitochondria

(C) ** Golgi bodies

(D) Centrioles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The discharge released by the Golgi bodies of the sperm cells migrates to the forming acrosome. At their surface, these vesicles fuse, with the acrosomal membrane, helping growth of acrosome. Although their location is in the nucleus, they are formed by the cell organelle, ‘Golgi bodies or golgi complex’.

Concept note-2: -The acrosome is a special kind of organelle with a cap-like structure that covers the anterior portion of the head of the spermatozoon. The acrosome is derived from the Golgi apparatus and contains digestive enzymes.

Concept note-3: -It is an organelle that develops over the anterior half of the head in the spermatozoa or sperm cells of many animals. It is a cap-like structure derived from the Golgi apparatus. It helps the sperm head to penetrate inside the layers of the egg.

Concept note-4: -In spermatogenesis, the Golgi apparatus is important for the formation of the acrosome, which is a sperm-specific organelle essential for fertilization.

Concept note-5: -The head of the sperm has a cap-like structure called the acrosome. It is derived from the Golgi apparatus.
