Acts as the cell’s “bones and muscles” by furnishing an internal framework that determines cell shape, supports other organelles

(A) Cytosol

(B) Cell Membrane

(C) ** Cytoskeleton

(D) Cytoplasm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Golgi apparatus (or, Golgi body) Cytoskeleton.

Concept note-2: -The cytoskeleton provides a structural framework for the cell, serving as a scaffold that determines cell shape and the general organization of the cytoplasm. In addition to playing this structural role, the cytoskeleton is responsible for cell movements.

Concept note-3: -As its name suggests, the cytoskeleton is like a cellular “skeleton.” It helps the cell maintain its shape and also helps to hold cell structures such as organelles in place within the cytoplasm. Figure 5.5. 2: Cytoskeleton. The cytoskeleton gives the cell an internal structure, like the frame of a house.

Concept note-4: -The cytoskeleton is involved in providing cell shape and maintaining internal organization of the cell. The endoplasmic reticulum is also involved in providing cell shape and maintaining internal organization by spreading almost all over the cell. Thus, the correct answer is option A.
