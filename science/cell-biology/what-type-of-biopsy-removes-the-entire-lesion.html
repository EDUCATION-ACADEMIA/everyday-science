What type of biopsy removes the entire lesion?

(A) aspiration

(B) internal

(C) ** excision

(D) incision

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During an excisional biopsy, the doctor removes an entire lump or an entire area of abnormal skin, including a portion of normal skin.

Concept note-2: -Excisional biopsy. During an excisional biopsy, an entire lump or area of skin that appears suspicious is removed.

Concept note-3: -A surgical procedure in which a cut is made through the skin to remove an entire lump or suspicious area so it can be checked under a microscope for signs of disease. A small amount of healthy tissue around the abnormal area may also be removed.

Concept note-4: -An excisional biopsy, also called a wide local incision, involves surgical removal of a tumor and some normal tissue around it.

Concept note-5: -An excisional biopsy uses a scalpel to remove all of the skin lesion, usually with some normal skin around it. The sample may include the full thickness of the skin along with fat below the skin.
