What type of cells may never undergo mitosis and make new cells?

(A) blood cells

(B) skin cells

(C) ** nerve cells

(D) stomach cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Skin cells, red blood cells or gut lining cells cannot undergo mitosis.

Concept note-2: -You read earlier that some cells in humans do not undergo mitosis. Two particular cells that reproduce through another method are sperm cells and egg cells, also called gametes. Instead of mitosis, gametes reproduce through a process called meiosis.

Concept note-3: -Neurons and muscle cells in adult humans do not have the ability to divide by mitosis, so they can not repair themselves and their cell cycle remains in the interphase.

Concept note-4: -Neurons lack the Centrioles and hence Mitosis is not possible and so they canâ€™t divide.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: Both differentiated cells and sex cells generally do not undergo mitosis past a certain phase of development. These differentiated cells include neurons, myocytes (muscle cells), keratinocytes (skin cells), and most blood cells, including B-cells, T-cells, and red blood cells.
