In the second stage of the demographic transition

(A) birth rates stay high but death rates start decreasing increasing population size

(B) birth rates and death rates are both low so population starts decreasing

(C) ** birth rates and death rates are high meaning high stable population

(D) birth rates and death rates are decreasing so population growth starts slowing down

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stage 2 of the Demographic Transition Model (DTM) is characterized by a rapid decrease in a countryâ€™s death rate while the birth rate remains high. As such, the total population of a country in Stage 2 will rise because births outnumber deaths, not because the birth rate is rising.

Concept note-2: -Demographic cycle First stage (High stationary): This stage is characterized by a high birth rate and high death rate which cancel each other and the population remains stationary.

Concept note-3: -Stage 1-High birth and death rates lead to slow population growth. Stage 2-The death rate falls, but the birth rate remains high, leading to faster population growth. Stage 3-The birth rate starts to fall, so population growth starts to slow.
