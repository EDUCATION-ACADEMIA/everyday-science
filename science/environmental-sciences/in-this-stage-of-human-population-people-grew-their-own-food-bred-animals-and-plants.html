In this stage of human population people grew their own food, bred animals and plants.

(A) artificial selection

(B) ** agricultural revolution

(C) industrial revolution

(D) hunter gatherers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Neolithic Period or the New Stone age is the period which is indicated with agriculture as its connection because in this time the introduction of cereal cultivation and animal domestication occurred. The people began to utilise cultivated land to grow different crops, advanced tools, pottery or constant shelters.

Concept note-2: -Mesolithic period The transitional period between the end of the Paleolithic and beginning of the Neolithic is called Mesolithic. It began about 10000BC and ended with the rise of agriculture.

Concept note-3: -On the basis of such evidence, one of the oldest transitions from hunting and gathering to agriculture has been identified as dating to between 14, 500 and 12, 000 bp in Southwest Asia.

Concept note-4: -Answer: The early humans were hunter-gatherers. Then, they began producing their food by cultivation of crops and also domesticated animals.

Concept note-5: -Neolithic Age Gordon Childe coined the term “Neolithic Revolution” in 1935 to describe the radical and important period of change in which humans began cultivating plants, breeding animals for food and forming permanent settlements. The advent of agriculture separated Neolithic people from their Paleolithic ancestors.
