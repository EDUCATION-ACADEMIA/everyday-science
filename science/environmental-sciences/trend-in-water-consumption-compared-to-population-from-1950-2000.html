Trend in water consumption compared to population from 1950-2000.

(A) population and water consumption both decreased

(B) ** population and water consumption both increased

(C) population increased and water consumption decreased

(D) population decreased and water consumption increased

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Water use has been increasing worldwide by about 1% per year since the 1980s, driven by a combination of population growth, socio-economic development and changing consumption patterns.

Concept note-2: -Looking forward, it is estimated that water demand will continue to rise. This is mainly ascribed to demand by industry and households, which taken together will represent an almost threefold increase by 2050. As our available water resources reach their physical limits, so water security may be adversely affected.

Concept note-3: -Irrigation water use increased by about 29 percent since 1950-it takes more water to grow food for our increasing population.

Concept note-4: -Due to increase in population, people move to cities which leads to building more shelters, for which people are clearing the forests by cutting down the trees i.e, deforestation. Due to deforestation water cycle gets affected thereby reducing the rainfall, and lack of rainfall is responsible for the shortage of water.
