Earth is covered by hundreds of types of ecosystems which are grouped by areas that have distinctive climates and organisms called ____

(A) Niches

(B) ** Biomes

(C) Benthic zones

(D) Habitats

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Terrestrial ecosystems can be further grouped into broad categories called biomes, based largely on climate. Examples of terrestrial biomes include tropical rain forests, savannas, deserts, coniferous forests, deciduous forests, and tundra. The map below shows the broad distribution of biomes on Earth.

Concept note-2: -There are five major types of biomes: aquatic, grassland, forest, desert, and tundra, though some of these biomes can be further divided into more specific categories, such as freshwater, marine, savanna, tropical rainforest, temperate rainforest, and taiga. Aquatic biomes include both freshwater and marine biomes.

Concept note-3: -A group of ecosystems that have similar climates and organisms is called a biome. Biotic and Abiotic Factors Ecosystems include biotic and abiotic factors.

Concept note-4: -The ocean is a huge body of saltwater that covers about 71 percent of Earthâ€™s surface. The planet has one global ocean, though oceanographers and the countries of the world have traditionally divided it into four distinct regions: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, and Arctic oceans.

Concept note-5: -We live in a terrestrial ecosystem. This is the ecosystem where organisms interact on landforms. Examples of terrestrial ecosystems include tundra, taigas, and tropical rainforests. Deserts, grasslands and temperate deciduous forests also constitute terrestrial ecosystems.
