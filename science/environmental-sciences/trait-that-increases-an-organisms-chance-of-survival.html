Trait that increases an organism’s chance of survival.

(A) natural selection

(B) ** adaptation

(C) evolution

(D) artificial selection

(E) resistance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The idea of natural selection is that traits that can be passed down allow organisms to adapt to the environment better than other organisms of the same species. This enables better survival and reproduction compared with other members of the species, leading to evolution.

Concept note-2: -In biology, an adaptation is ANY inherited characteristic that increases an organism’s chance for survival.

Concept note-3: -Adaptation can protect animals from predators or from harsh weather. Many birds can hide in the tall grass and weeds and insects can change their colour to blend into the surroundings. This makes it difficult for predators to seek them out for food.

Concept note-4: -Adaptation. A trait that improves an organism’s ability to survive and reproduce in an environment. Fitness.

Concept note-5: -A trait is an aspect of the whole or of a certain portion of the developmental pattern of the organism. An adaptive trait is, then, an aspect of the developmental pattern which facilitates the survival and/or reproduction of its carrier in a certain succession of environments.
