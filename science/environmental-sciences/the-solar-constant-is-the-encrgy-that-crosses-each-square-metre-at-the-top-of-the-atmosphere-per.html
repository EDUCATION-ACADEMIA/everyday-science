EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The solar constant is the energy which reaches the earth upper surface per second on 1 m2. It is usually taken to be 1, 388 watts per meter square.

Concept note-2: -The solar irradiance intercepted by the earth at the top of the atmosphere, the mean solar constant, is quite stable with an observed mean value of 1365 W/m2 ± 0.3%.

Concept note-3: -The solar constant is the incident ray of solar energy per unit area per second on the earth surface.

Concept note-4: -A solar constant is a measurement of the solar electromagnetic radiation available in a meter squared at Earth’s distance from the sun. The solar constant is used to quantify the rate at which energy is received upon a unit surface such as a solar panel.
