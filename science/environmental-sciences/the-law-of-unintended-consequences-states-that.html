The Law of Unintended Consequences states that

(A) ** actions of people and governments have effects that are unanticipated.

(B) everything can always be predicted ahead of time.

(C) things which look good on paper work out well in reality.

(D) disasters can usually be avoided with enough money and time planning.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The law of unintended consequences, often cited but rarely defined, is that actions of people-and especially of government-always have effects that are unanticipated or unintended.

Concept note-2: -1. “Three strikes” laws may actually be increasing the murder rate, and not decreasing it. 2. Seat belt laws increase the number of car accidents, and increase pedestrian and cyclist deaths.

Concept note-3: -The law of unintended consequences refers to how economic decisions may have effects that are unexpected. Usually, this refers to an economic law which distorts consumer or producer behaviour in a way that is not expected.
