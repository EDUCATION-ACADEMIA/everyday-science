The Arctic National Wildlife Refuge is a section of federally-owned land in Alaska. What activities would not normally be allowed in this area?

(A) Hiking and camping

(B) ** Commercial logging

(C) ** Drilling for oil

(D) There are no commercial restrictions for wildlife refuges.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The arctic national wildlife refuge is a section of federally-owned land in Alaska. The activities that would not generally be allowed in this area are commercial logging and drilling for oil.

Concept note-2: -Ecological ethics stresses the intrinsic value of human life but pushes us to consider the intrinsic value of animals, plants, and ecosystems.

Concept note-3: -Which of these events during the modern environmentalism era raised awareness of the problem of hazardous waste disposal? The hitch hetchy dam construction was one of the first debates between the anthropocentriist and ecocentrist environmental philosophies.

Concept note-4: -What is Environmental Science? Environmental science is the dynamic, interdisciplinary study of the interaction of living and non-living parts of the environment, with special focus on the impact of humans on the environment.

Concept note-5: -Environmental science is also referred to as an interdisciplinary field because it incorporates information and ideas from multiple disciplines. Within the natural sciences, such fields as biology, chemistry, and geology are included in environmental science.
