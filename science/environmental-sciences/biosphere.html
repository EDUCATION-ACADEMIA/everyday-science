Biosphere

(A) ** ▶part of Earth that contains all ecosystem

(B) a group of various species that live in the same place

(C) all of the members of the same species that live in the same place

(D) a group of organism that can mate to produce fertile offspring

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The biosphere is made up of the parts of Earth where life exists-all ecosystems. The biosphere extends from the deepest root systems of trees, to the dark environments of ocean trenches, to lush rain forests, high mountaintops, and transition zones like this one, where ocean and terrestrial ecosystems meet.

Concept note-2: -It is made up of the lithosphere’s surface, a lower portion of the atmosphere, and the hydrosphere. The biosphere is the world’s ecological system as a whole. It encompasses all forms of life on Earth as well as all habitats capable of sustaining life. There are many biomes that make up the biosphere.

Concept note-3: -The sum of all the ecosystems of the world. It is both the collection of organisms living on the Earth and the space that they occupy on part of the Earth’s crust (the lithosphere), in the oceans (the hydrosphere) and in the atmosphere. The biosphere is all the planet’s ecosystems.

Concept note-4: -There are eight major terrestrial biomes: tropical rainforests, savannas, subtropical deserts, chaparral, temperate grasslands, temperate forests, boreal forests, and Arctic tundra.

Concept note-5: -Complete answer: On the earth, the biosphere is the largest ecosystem.
