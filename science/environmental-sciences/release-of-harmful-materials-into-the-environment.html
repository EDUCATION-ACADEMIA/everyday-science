Release of harmful materials into the environment

(A) ** pollution

(B) biodegradable

(C) ecology

(D) agriculture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials into the environment. These harmful materials are called pollutants. Pollutants can be natural, such as volcanic ash. They can also be created by human activity, such as trash or runoff produced by factories.

Concept note-2: -Air Pollutants. The World Health Organization (WHO) reports on six major air pollutants, namely particle pollution, ground-level ozone, carbon monoxide, sulfur oxides, nitrogen oxides, and lead. Air pollution can have a disastrous effect on all components of the environment, including groundwater, soil, and air.

Concept note-3: -The majority of these pollutants are emitted through human activities like burning fossil fuels, vehicle exhaust fumes and emissions from agriculture and industry.

Concept note-4: -The Burning of Fossil Fuels Most of the air pollution takes place due to the incomplete burning of fossil fuels. These include coal, oil, and gasoline to produce energy for electricity or transportation. The release of CO at a high level indicates how much fossil fuel is burned.

Concept note-5: -Air Pollution. Water Pollution. Soil Pollution. Noise Pollution.
