Biotic and abiotic factors refer to what level of organization:

(A) population

(B) community

(C) ** ecosystem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: â€“The environment is a unique creation that encompasses all living plants, animals, and microorganisms (biotic factors) in a specific region, as well as all non-living physical (abiotic) factors.-The environment is the lowest level of organisation of which nonliving (abiotic) elements are considered.

Concept note-2: -Ecosystem: An ecosystem consists of all the biotic and abiotic factors that influence that community.

Concept note-3: -For the species, population and community, they only contain biotic or living forms because they are a group of living organisms living together in the same place, while the ecosystem contains both biotic and abiotic forms because this is where the two forms interact with one another.
