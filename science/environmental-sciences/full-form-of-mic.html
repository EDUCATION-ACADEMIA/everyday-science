Concept note-1: -What Is MIC? MIC (methyl isocyanate) is a volatile, colorless liquid that is extremely flammable, and potentially explosive when mixed with air. MIC reacts with water, giving off heat and producing methylamine and carbon dioxide. The liquid and vapor are toxic when inhaled, ingested, or exposed to the eyes or skin.

Concept note-2: -MIC is also manufactured from N-methylformamide and air. Because of its high reactivity, MIC is used as an intermediate in organic synthesis, most notably in the production of carbamate-based pesticides.
