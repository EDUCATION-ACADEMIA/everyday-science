There are forty -six chromosomes in a normal human body cell. Compare the chromosome numbers given below with the special structure in the human body. 1. Ovum 23 2, Sperm 46 3, Kidney cells 46 4, Urinary bladder 23 Which of the above pairs are correctly matched?

(A) 1,2 and 4

(B) 2,3 and 4

(C) ** 1 and 3

(D) 1,3 and 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Normal human body cells are diploid. The basic chromosome number of human cell is n=23. So, a normal cell has 2n= 46 chromosomes out of which 44 are autosomes and 2 are sex chromosomes. Thus, the correct answer is D.

Concept note-2: -Each daughter cell will have half of the original 46 chromosomes, or 23 chromosomes. Each chromosome consists of 2 sister chromatids. The daughter cells now move in to the third and final phase of meiosis: meiosis II.

Concept note-3: -In plants and animals (including humans), chromosomes reside in the nucleus of cells. Humans have 22 pairs of numbered chromosomes (autosomes) and one pair of sex chromosomes (XX or XY), for a total of 46.

Concept note-4: -Healthy humans have 46 chromosomes, 23 from the sperm and 23 from the egg. An embryo with the wrong number of chromosomes is usually miscarried, or develops disorders such as Downâ€™s syndrome, which is caused by an extra copy of chromosome 21.

Concept note-5: -Normally, each cell in the human body has 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total chromosomes). Half come from the mother; the other half come from the father. Two of the chromosomes (the X and the Y chromosome) determine your sex as male or female when you are born.
