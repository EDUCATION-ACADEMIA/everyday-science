What is the main function of bone marrow?

(A) Supporting internal organs

(B) Keeping the spine straight

(C) Storing minerals

(D) ** Producing blood cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bone marrow makes the components of your blood that you need to survive. Bone marrow produces red blood cells that carry oxygen, white blood cells that prevent infection and platelets that control bleeding. The absence of bone marrow can be fatal since it’s an essential part of your body.

Concept note-2: -Bone marrow is a spongy substance found in the center of the bones. It manufactures bone marrow stem cells and other substances, which in turn produce blood cells. Each type of blood cell made by the bone marrow has an important job. Red blood cells carry oxygen to tissues in the body.

Concept note-3: -Red bone marrow contains blood stem cells that can become red blood cells, white blood cells, or platelets. Yellow bone marrow is made mostly of fat and contains stem cells that can become cartilage, fat, or bone cells.

Concept note-4: -The function of bone marrow is to produce new red blood cells to replace old ones that are damaged. In the body, red blood cells carry oxygen to the tissue (a group of cells that performs a particular function) of the body.

Concept note-5: -Blood cells are made in the bone marrow. The bone marrow is the soft, spongy material in the center of the bones. It produces about 95% of the body’s blood cells.
