Short bones would most likely be found in which part of the body?

(A) Skull

(B) Arms and Legs

(C) ** Wrists and Ankles

(D) Spin and Knee Cap

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They consist primarily of spongy bone, which is covered by a thin layer of compact bone . Short bones include the bones of the wrist and ankle.

Concept note-2: -Short bones are shaped roughly as a cube and contain mostly spongy bone. The outside surface is comprised of a thin layer of compact bone. Short bones are located in the hands and feet. The patella (kneecap) is also considered a short bone.

Concept note-3: -Short bones in the human body are often cube-like, their length, width, and height are all about the same. Short bones include the carpal bones of the hands that allow movement of the wrist, and the tarsal bones of the feet that allow movement of the ankle.

Concept note-4: -Short Bones Are Cube-shaped The carpals in the wrist (scaphoid, lunate, triquetral, hamate, pisiform, capitate, trapezoid, and trapezium) and the tarsals in the ankles (calcaneus, talus, navicular, cuboid, lateral cuneiform, intermediate cuneiform, and medial cuneiform) are examples of short bones.

Concept note-5: -the fibula, the smaller bone of the lower leg, which forms the outside part of the ankle. the talus, a small bone between the tibia and fibula and the calcaneus, or heel bone.
