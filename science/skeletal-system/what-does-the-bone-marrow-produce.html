What does the bone marrow produce?

(A) ** Blood cells

(B) Nutrients

(C) Mineral

(D) Vitamins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bone marrow produces red blood cells that carry oxygen, white blood cells that prevent infection and platelets that control bleeding. The absence of bone marrow can be fatal since it’s an essential part of your body.

Concept note-2: -Bone marrow produces many types of white blood cells. These are necessary for a healthy immune system. They prevent and fight infections. The main types of white blood cells, or leukocytes, are as follows.

Concept note-3: -Bone marrow is a spongy substance found in the center of the bones. It manufactures bone marrow stem cells and other substances, which in turn produce blood cells. Each type of blood cell made by the bone marrow has an important job. Red blood cells carry oxygen to tissues in the body.

Concept note-4: -The human bone marrow is one of the body’s largest organs occupying the central regions of virtually all long and flat bones. Consisting of a mixture of fat and hematopoietic precursor, the marrow typically produces 500 billion mature blood cells daily (Fliedner et al., 2002).

Concept note-5: -Erythropoiesis is red blood cell (erythrocyte) production. Your bone marrow makes most of your red blood cells. Once they’re fully mature, they’re released into your bloodstream, where they transport oxygen throughout your body.
