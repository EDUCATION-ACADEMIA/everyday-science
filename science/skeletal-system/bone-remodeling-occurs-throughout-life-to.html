Bone remodeling occurs throughout life to

(A) repair fractures

(B) recycle bone

(C) ** both of these are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The remodeling process occurs throughout life and becomes dominant by the time that bone reaches its peak mass (typically by the early 20s). Remodeling continues throughout life so that most of the adult skeleton is replaced about every 10 years. Both genes and the environment contribute to bone health.

Concept note-2: -Bone remodeling is essential for adult bone homeostasis. It comprises two phases: bone formation and resorption.

Concept note-3: -Bone remodeling refers to the renewal process whereby small pockets of old bone, disposed throughout the skeleton and separated from others geographically as well as chronologically, are replaced by new bone throughout adult life. The process is such that the entire adult human skeleton is replaced in 10 years.

Concept note-4: -Bone remodeling is the result of two opposite activities, the production of new bone matrix by osteoblasts and the destruction of old bone by osteoclasts.
