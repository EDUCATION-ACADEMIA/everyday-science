Which bones are not fully movable?

(A) the hip bones

(B) the leg bones

(C) ** the bones of the skull

(D) the arm bones

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The skull, as we know it, is made up of 22 bones (28 if auditory ossicles are included). But out of all these bones, only the mandible is movable.

Concept note-2: -The adult skull is made up of 22 bones; 21 of them are immovable and the 22nd one is the mandible, the lower jaw, and is the only moveable bone of the skull. The cranial bones form the cranial cavity which holds and protects the brain.

Concept note-3: -The only bone in your skull that forms freely movable joints is your mandible, or jawbone.

Concept note-4: -Types of joints Immovable – the two or more bones are in close contact, but no movement can occur – for example, the bones of the skull. The joints of the skull are called sutures.

Concept note-5: -The only movable joint in our head is the joint between the skull and lower jaw(mandible).
