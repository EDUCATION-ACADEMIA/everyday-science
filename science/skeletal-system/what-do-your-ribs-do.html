What do your ribs do

(A) ** Protects Chest Cavity

(B) Protects ankle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rib cage surrounds the lungs and the heart, serving as an important means of bony protection for these vital organs.In total, the rib cage consists of the 12 thoracic vertebrae and the 24 ribs, in addition to the sternum.

Concept note-2: -The ribs are connected to the sternum with a strong, somewhat flexible material called cartilage. The rib cage help protects the organs in the chest, such as the heart and lungs, from damage.

Concept note-3: -A Rib cage is a bony structure which is attached to the vertebral column and sternum in humans and it is helpful in the protection of heart and lungs. It has 24 ribs in 12 pairs.

Concept note-4: -The ribs and sternum make up the ribcage. The rib cage protects major vital organs such as the heart, lungs, and liver from injury. The rib cage has 24 ribs.

Concept note-5: -The ribs protect the vital organs of the torso, particularly the heart and lungs, by wrapping around from the thoracic vertebrae of the spine in the back to the sternum in the front.
