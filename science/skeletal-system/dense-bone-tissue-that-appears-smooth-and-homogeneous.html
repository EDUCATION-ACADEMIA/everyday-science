Dense bone tissue that appears smooth and homogeneous:

(A) adipose tissue

(B) ** compact bone

(C) spongy bone

(D) hyaline cartilage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Compact bone is smooth, homogenous, and dense. Spongy bone has lots of needle like pieces called trabeculae and lots of spaces. We classify bones by their shape. Long-longer than wide, compact bone outside, some have spongy bone on the interior.

Concept note-2: -Cortical bone tissue gives bone its smooth, dense, solid appearance. It accounts for about 80 percent of the total bone mass of the adult skeleton. Spongy bone tissue fills part or all of the interior of many bones. As its name suggests, spongy bone is porous like a sponge, containing an irregular network of spaces.

Concept note-3: -Inside the diaphysis is the medullary cavity, which is filled with yellow bone marrow in an adult. The outer walls of the diaphysis (cortex, cortical bone) are composed of dense and hard compact bone, a form of osseous tissue.

Concept note-4: -compact bone, also called cortical bone, dense bone in which the bony matrix is solidly filled with organic ground substance and inorganic salts, leaving only tiny spaces (lacunae) that contain the osteocytes, or bone cells.

Concept note-5: -What does it mean that compact bone is homogeneous and spongy bone is heterogeneous? Compact bone is homogeneous because it protects and supports. Spongy bone is heterogeneous because it had many open spaces and is a meshwork of supporting fibers called trabeculae.
