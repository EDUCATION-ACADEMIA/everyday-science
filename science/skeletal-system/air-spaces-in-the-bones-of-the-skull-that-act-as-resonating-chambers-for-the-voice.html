Air spaces in the bones of the skull that act as resonating chambers for the voice

(A) Sutures

(B) Fontanels

(C) Patells

(D) ** Sinuses

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These sinuses, which have the same names as the bones in which they are located, surround the nasal cavity and open into it. They function to reduce the weight of the skull, to produce mucus, and to influence voice quality by acting as resonating chambers.

Concept note-2: -Paranasal sinuses are named after the bones that contain them: frontal (the lower forehead), maxillary (cheekbones), ethmoid (beside the upper nose), and sphenoid (behind the nose).

Concept note-3: -The primary function of the ethmoid sinus, like all the sinus cavities in the skull, is to provide lubrication (mucus) to the inner nose. In addition to creating mucus, the sinuses-including the ethmoid sinus-reduce the skull’s overall weight and make one’s voice more resonant as they grow in size during puberty.

Concept note-4: -The paranasal sinuses (the hollow spaces in the skull and facial bones around the nose) are air-filled cavities within the frontal, ethmoidal, sphenoidal, and maxillary bones.

Concept note-5: -Structure of the paranasal sinuses The paranasal sinuses are hollow spaces filled with air, located around the nose inside the skull. The paranasal sinuses are connected to one another and to the nasal cavity.
