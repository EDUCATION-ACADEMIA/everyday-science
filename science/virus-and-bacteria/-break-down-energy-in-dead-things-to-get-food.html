____ break down energy in dead things to get food.

(A) Protists

(B) ** Fungi

(C) Animals

(D) Plants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Decomposers play a critical role in the flow of energy through an ecosystem. They break apart dead organisms into simpler inorganic materials, making nutrients available to primary producers.

Concept note-2: -This process is called decomposition and is essential for releasing nutrients back into the environment that can be used by other organisms. Decomposers like bacteria and fungi take organic matter and break it down into simpler components like carbon dioxide and water.

Concept note-3: -Fungi decompose organic matter by releasing enzymes to break down the decaying material, after which they absorb the nutrients in the decaying material. Hyphae are used to break down matter and absorb nutrients and are also used in reproduction.

Concept note-4: -A decomposer is an organism that decomposes, or breaks down, organic material such as the remains of dead organisms. Decomposers include bacteria and fungi. These organisms carry out the process of decomposition, which all living organisms undergo after death.

Concept note-5: -All fungi are heterotrophic, which means that they get the energy they need to live from other organisms. Like animals, fungi extract the energy stored in the bonds of organic compounds such as sugar and protein from living or dead organisms. Many of these compounds can also be recycled for further use.
