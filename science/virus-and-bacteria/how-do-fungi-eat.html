How do fungi eat?

(A) ** They decompose and collect nutrients from dead animals

(B) By hunting insects

(C) By photosynthesis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some kinds of fungi, such as mushrooms, look like plants. But fungi do not contain chlorophyll, the pigment that green plants use to make their own food with the energy of sunlight. Instead, fungi get all their nutrients from dead materials that they break down with special enzymes.

Concept note-2: -Fungi secrete enzymes that can break down complex organic compounds like carbohydrates and proteins into simpler components with the release of energy. These fungal decomposers, alongside their saprobic bacterial allies, absorb only a small amount of these nutrients and energy for their own use.

Concept note-3: -Fungi and bacteria are not restricted to decomposing leaves and other plant materials. They will decompose any dead organic matter, whether it is a cardboard box, paint, glue, pair of jeans, a leather jacket or jet fuel.

Concept note-4: -Detritivores are organisms that feed orally on dead matter, to gain nutrients and energy. Examples of detritivores: millipedes, earthworms, crabs, flies, etc. Decomposers act on the dead matter, e.g. by secreting the enzymes and digesting the matter externally.

Concept note-5: -Saprotrophic fungi obtain their food from dead organic material; parasitic fungi do so by feeding on living organisms (usually plants), thus causing disease.
