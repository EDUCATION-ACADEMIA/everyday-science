The theory of evolution by natural selection means that what is changing over time?

(A) ** The inherited traits of a population.

(B) The acquired traits of a population.

(C) Hair color and eye color

(D) Attractive features.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The theory of natural selection was explored by 19th-century naturalist Charles Darwin. Natural selection explains how genetic traits of a species may change over time. This may lead to speciation, the formation of a distinct new species.

Concept note-2: -Natural selection is a mechanism of evolution. Organisms that are more adapted to their environment are more likely to survive and pass on the genes that aided their success. This process causes species to change and diverge over time.

Concept note-3: -Evolution: Evolution consists of changes in the heritable traits of a population of organisms as successive generations replace one another.

Concept note-4: -Biological evolution refers to the cumulative changes that occur in a population over time. These changes are produced at the genetic level as organismsâ€™ genes mutate and/or recombine in different ways during reproduction and are passed on to future generations.

Concept note-5: -Directional selection occurs when individuals with traits on one side of the mean in their population survive better or reproduce more than those on the other. It has been demonstrated many times in natural populations, using both observational and experimental approaches.
