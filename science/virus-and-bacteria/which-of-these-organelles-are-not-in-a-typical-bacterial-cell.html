Which of these organelles are not in a typical bacterial cell?

(A) Ribosome

(B) ** Endoplasmic Reticulum

(C) Cytoplasm

(D) Cell wall

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bacteria do not have any membrane-bound organelle including the endoplasmic reticulum. Prokaryotes like bacteria and archaea lack any membrane-bound organelle like ER, Golgi bodies, mitochondria, etc.

Concept note-2: -Complete answer: No, bacteria do not have endoplasmic reticulum because bacteria is a prokaryotic organism that lacks member bound organelles such as the nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, mitochondria, lysosomes, Golgi apparatus, etc.

Concept note-3: -Mitochondria is a membrane bound cell organelle. Such membrane bound cell organelles are absent in prokaryotic cells like bacteria.

Concept note-4: -The correct option is C Nucleus.

Concept note-5: -Final answer: Endoplasmic reticulum is not a site of energy generation.
