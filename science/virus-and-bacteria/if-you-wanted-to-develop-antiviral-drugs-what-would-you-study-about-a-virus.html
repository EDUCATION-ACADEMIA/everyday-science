If you wanted to develop antiviral drugs what would you study about a virus?

(A) Protein capsid used by the virus

(B) ** mechanism of how the virus infects the host

(C) DNA sequence of the virus

(D) The host cell which it infects

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Antiviral drug design could, in principle, be targeted at either viral proteins or cellular proteins. The first approach is likely to yield more specific, less toxic compounds, with a narrow spectrum of antiviral activity and a higher likelihood of resistance developing.

Concept note-2: -Mechanism of resistance Antiviral drug-resistant strains have signature mutations in the reverse transcriptase domains of the viral polymerase gene, with most substitutions occurring in domains B, C, and D. Resistance mutations alter the interaction between HBV polymerase and drug [164].

Concept note-3: -Name of FDA approved drugs: Atazanavir, darunavir, fosamprenavir, ritonavir, saquinavir, tipranavir Integrase inhibitor (II): Block the action of integrase, preventing the viral genome from inserting itself into the DNA of a host cell.
