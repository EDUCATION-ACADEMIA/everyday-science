Define germs

(A) ** tiny organisms, or living things, that can cause disease

(B) an infection that causes a fever

(C) handwashing

(D) medicine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term “germs” refers to the microscopic bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa that can cause disease. Washing hands well and often is the best way to prevent germs from leading to infections and sickness.

Concept note-2: -Germs are microorganisms. This means that they can be seen only through a microscope. They can be found everywhere-in the air, soil, and water. There are also germs on your skin and in your body. Many germs live in and on our bodies without causing harm.

Concept note-3: -Germs are found in air and water. 2. Germs are found in our hands, nails and in the ridges.

Concept note-4: -A pathogen is an organism that causes disease. Your body is naturally full of microbes. However, these microbes only cause a problem if your immune system is weakened or if they manage to enter a normally sterile part of your body. Pathogens are different and can cause disease upon entering the body.

Concept note-5: -AIDS. Common cold. Ebola. Genital herpes. Influenza. Measles. Chickenpox and shingles. Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19)
