Which is the correct order of the steps of the lytic cycle?

(A) Adsorption; replication; entry; assembly; release

(B) ** Attachment; entry; replication; assembly; release

(C) Attachment; entry; assembly; replication; exit

(D) Entry; adsorption; replication; assembly; release

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So this is the correct order first it does attachment, then entry After this that is synthesis and after synthesis, assembly and the last release.

Concept note-2: -The lytic cycle, which is also commonly referred to as the “reproductive cycle” of the bacteriophage, is a six-stage cycle. The six stages are: attachment, penetration, transcription, biosynthesis, maturation, and lysis.

Concept note-3: -Viral replication involves six steps: attachment, penetration, uncoating, replication, assembly, and release. During attachment and penetration, the virus attaches itself to a host cell and injects its genetic material into it.
