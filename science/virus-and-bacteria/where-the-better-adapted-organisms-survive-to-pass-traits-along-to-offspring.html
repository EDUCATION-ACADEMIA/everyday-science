Where the better adapted organisms survive to pass traits along to offspring.

(A) Evolution

(B) ** Natural Selection

(C) Extinction

(D) Artifical Selection

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Natural selection is a mechanism of evolution. Organisms that are more adapted to their environment are more likely to survive and pass on the genes that aided their success. This process causes species to change and diverge over time.

Concept note-2: -Individuals with adaptive traits-traits that give them some advantage-are more likely to survive and reproduce. These individuals then pass the adaptive traits on to their offspring. Over time, these advantageous traits become more common in the population.

Concept note-3: -Natural selection is a process where organisms with beneficial and advantageous traits survive better than others. As generations pass, the species will have many organisms that have that advantageous trait.

Concept note-4: -The idea of natural selection is that traits that can be passed down allow organisms to adapt to the environment better than other organisms of the same species. This enables better survival and reproduction compared with other members of the species, leading to evolution.

Concept note-5: -Natural selection can act on traits determined by different alleles of a single gene, or on polygenic traits (traits determined by many genes).
