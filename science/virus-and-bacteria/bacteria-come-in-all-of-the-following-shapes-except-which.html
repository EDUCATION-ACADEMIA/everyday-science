Bacteria come in all of the following shapes EXCEPT which?

(A) spiral

(B) rod-like

(C) ** rectangular

(D) spherical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The correct option is c, oval. The cell shape is the characteristics of the bacterial species and bacteria are grouped under four categories based on their cell shape; spherical (coccus), rod-shaped (bacillus), comma-shaped (vibrium) and spiral (helical or spirillum).

Concept note-2: -Bacteria have distinct cell body shapes, ranging from spheres (cocci) to rods (bacilli) of various curvatures and helicities and to more exotic shapes, such as stars, formed by elaboration of prosthecae through polar growth.

Concept note-3: -In bacteria, the cell wall forms a rigid structure of uniform thickness around the cell and is responsible for the characteristic shape of the cell (rod, coccus, or spiral). Inside the cell wall (or rigid peptidoglycan layer) is the plasma (cytoplasmic) membrane; this is usually closely apposed to the wall layer.

Concept note-4: -Bacterial cells do not have an organized nucleus.
