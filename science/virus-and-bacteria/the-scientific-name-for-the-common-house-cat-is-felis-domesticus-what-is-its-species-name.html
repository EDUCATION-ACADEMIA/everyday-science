The scientific name for the common house cat is Felis domesticus. What is its species name?

(A) Felis

(B) Animalia

(C) ** domesticus

(D) house cat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cat (Felis catus) is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family.

Concept note-2: -Definitions of Felis domesticus. any domesticated member of the genus Felis. synonyms: Felis catus, domestic cat, house cat.

Concept note-3: -Summary. The domestic cat (Felis catus or Felis silvestris catus) is a small, usually furry, domesticated, and carnivorous mammal. It is often called the housecat when kept as an indoor pet, or simply the cat when there is no need to distinguish it from other felids and felines.

Concept note-4: -Domesticus (Latin for belonging to the house), is found in the binomial or trinomial names of many species or subspecies of animals commonly found in or around the home. Examples include: Domesticus (Roman Empire), a member of the Domestici, an elite guard unit of the late Roman Empire.

Concept note-5: -Felis domesticus or Felis catus refers to the common housecat, while Felis concolor refer to a Mountain lion.
