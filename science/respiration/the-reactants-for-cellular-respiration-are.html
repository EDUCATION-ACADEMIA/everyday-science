the reactants for cellular respiration are

(A) oxygen and water

(B) food and water

(C) ** glucose and oxygen

(D) energy and carbon dioxide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In cellular respiration, glucose and oxygen react to form ATP. Water and carbon dioxide are released as byproducts. The three stages of aerobic cellular respiration are glycolysis (an anaerobic process), the Krebs cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation.

Concept note-2: -The Reactants Oxygen and glucose are both reactants of cellular respiration. Oxygen enters the body when an organism breathes. Glucose enters the body when an organism eats.

Concept note-3: -Cellular respiration converts oxygen and glucose into water and carbon dioxide. Water and carbon dioxide are by-products and ATP is energy that is transformed from the process.

Concept note-4: -Glucose is the molecule normally used for respiration-it is the main respiratory substrate . Glucose is oxidised to release its energy, which is then stored in ATP molecules. Respiration is a series of chemical reactions, but this equation summarises the overall process.

Concept note-5: -The products of photosynthesis are glucose and oxygen, and the reactants are water and carbon dioxide.
