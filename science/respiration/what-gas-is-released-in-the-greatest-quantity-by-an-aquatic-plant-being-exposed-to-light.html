What gas is released, in the greatest quantity, by an aquatic plant being exposed to light?

(A) ** oxygen

(B) carbon dioxide

(C) water

(D) nitrogen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the presence of sunlight, these produce oxygen through photosynthesis and release this oxygen into the pond water. At night and on very cloudy days, algae and submerged plants remove oxygen from the water for respiration.

Concept note-2: -Dissolved-oxygen gas is released as a by-product. In aquatic environments, free-floating microscopic plants known as algae, and larger submersed plants (macrophytes), release oxygen directly into the water where it is used by animals and other organisms, including the plants themselves.

Concept note-3: -Aquatic Plants and Photosynthesis Plants, including aquatic plants, produce oxygen, and they also use oxygen. Concept note-4: -During the day, aquatic plants absorb CO2, producing O2, and during the night they absorb O2 and produce CO2.

Concept note-5: -Plants do require oxygen to respire, the process in return gives out carbon dioxide.
