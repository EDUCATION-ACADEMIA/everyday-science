The amount of energy needed to raise the temperatiure of one gram of water by one degree of Celsius

(A) Carbohydrates

(B) Glucose

(C) Vitamins

(D) ** Calorie

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -One calorie is defined as the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of one gram of water by one degree Celsius at a pressure of one atmosphere. It is a unit of energy also.

Concept note-2: -Quantitative experiments show that 4.18 Joules of heat energy are required to raise the temperature of 1g of water by 1°C. Thus, a liter (1000g) of water that increased from 24 to 25°C has absorbed 4.18 J/g°C x 1000g x 1°C or 4180 Joules of energy.

Concept note-3: -One kilocalorie (1 kcal or 1000 calories) is the amount of heat (energy) needed to raise the temperature of one kg of water by one degree Celsius (°C). The SI standard unit for energy is Joule (J).

Concept note-4: -The kilocalorie, or kilogram calorie, is the quantity of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of pure water 1°C; it is equal to 1, 000 cal.
