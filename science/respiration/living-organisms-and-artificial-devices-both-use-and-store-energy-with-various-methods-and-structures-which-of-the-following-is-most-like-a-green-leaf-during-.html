Living organisms and artificial devices both use and store energy with various methods and structures. Which of the following is MOST like a green leaf during the daytime?

(A) a person running along a track

(B) ** a solar collector charging a battery

(C) a person eating a hamburger at lunch time

(D) a wind turbine generating electric current as it spins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -photosynthesis, the process by which green plants and certain other organisms transform light energy into chemical energy. During photosynthesis in green plants, light energy is captured and used to convert water, carbon dioxide, and minerals into oxygen and energy-rich organic compounds.

Concept note-2: -During photosynthesis, plants trap light energy with their leaves. Plants use the energy of the sun to change water and carbon dioxide into a sugar called glucose. Glucose is used by plants for energy and to make other substances like cellulose and starch.

Concept note-3: -Plants carry out photosynthesis only during the day and respiration only at night.

Concept note-4: -This process is called photosynthesis and occurs in the chloroplast of the plant cell. Plants take in carbon dioxide through tiny openings or pores in their leaves called stomata.
