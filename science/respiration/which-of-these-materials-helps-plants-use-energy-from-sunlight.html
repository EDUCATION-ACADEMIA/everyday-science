Which of these materials helps plants use energy from sunlight?

(A) roots

(B) soil

(C) ** chlorophyll

(D) glucose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The chlorophyll absorbs energy from the light waves, which is converted into chemical energy in the form of the molecules ATP and NADPH.

Concept note-2: -Most plants contain a special colored chemical or pigment called chlorophyll that is used in photosynthesis. Chlorophyll is what absorbs the sun’s energy and turns it into chemical energy.

Concept note-3: -Chlorophyll’s job in a plant is to absorb light-usually sunlight. The energy absorbed from light is transferred to two kinds of energy-storing molecules. Through photosynthesis, the plant uses the stored energy to convert carbon dioxide (absorbed from the air) and water into glucose, a type of sugar.

Concept note-4: -Chlorophyll is the pigment that gives plants their green color, and is an essential component of photosynthesis whereby plants derive their energy for metabolism, growth, and reproductive processes.

Concept note-5: -It channels the energy of sunlight into chemical energy, converting it through the process of photosynthesis. In photosynthesis, chlorophyll absorbs energy to transform carbon dioxide and water into carbohydrates and oxygen.
