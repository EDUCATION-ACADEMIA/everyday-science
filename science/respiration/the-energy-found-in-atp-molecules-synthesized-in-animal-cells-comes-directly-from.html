The energy found in ATP molecules synthesized in animal cells comes directly from

(A) sunlight

(B) ** organic molecules

(C) minerals

(D) inorganic molecules

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Because the energy to drive ATP synthesis in mitochondria ultimately derives from the oxidative breakdown of food molecules, the phosphorylation of ADP to form ATP that is driven by electron transport in the mitochondrion is known as oxidative phosphorylation.

Concept note-2: -It is the creation of ATP from ADP using energy from sunlight, and occurs during photosynthesis. ATP is also formed from the process of cellular respiration in the mitochondria of a cell. This can be through aerobic respiration, which requires oxygen, or anaerobic respiration, which does not.

Concept note-3: -Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is an organic compound that provides energy to drive many processes in living cells, such as muscle contraction, nerve impulse propagation, condensate dissolution, and chemical synthesis.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is (a) mitochondria. Mitochondria are organelles that carry out cellular respiration, a metabolic process that breaks down organic compounds like glucose in order to release energy that is then used to produce ATP.

Concept note-5: -The ATP synthase is a mitochondrial enzyme localized in the inner membrane, where it catalyzes the synthesis of ATP from ADP and phosphate, driven by a flux of protons across a gradient generated by electron transfer from the proton chemically positive to the negative side.
