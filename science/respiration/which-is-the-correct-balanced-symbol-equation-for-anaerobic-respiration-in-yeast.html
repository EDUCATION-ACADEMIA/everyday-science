Which is the correct, balanced, symbol equation for anaerobic respiration in yeast?

(A) C6H12O6 + 6 O2 → 6 CO2 + 6 H2O

(B) C6H12O6 → 2 C3H6O3

(C) ** C6H12O6 → 2 C2H5OH + 2 CO2

(D) 6 CO2 + 6 H2O → C6H12O6 + 6 O2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -C6H12O6 ⟶ 2C2H5OH + 2CO2 + 2ATP.

Concept note-2: -The anaerobic respiration equation depends on which fermentation pathway is used by the living organism. For anaerobic respiration in animals, the equation is: C6H12O6→2C3H6O3+energy C 6 H 12 O 6 → 2 C 3 H 6 O 3 + e n e r g y .

Concept note-3: -Dissolved nutrients like glucose are slowly burnt (oxidized) with the help of oxygen and energy is released in the form of ATP. Waste materials like CO2 and water vapours are produced during this process. Cellular respiration can be summarized as follows: C6H12O6 + 6 O2 → 6 CO2 + 6 H2O + Energy (38ATP)

Concept note-4: -The photosynthesis equation is as follows: 6CO2 + 6H20 + (energy) → C6H12O6 + 6O2 Carbon dioxide + water + energy from light produces glucose and oxygen.
