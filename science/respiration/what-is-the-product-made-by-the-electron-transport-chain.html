What is the product made by the electron transport chain?

(A) ** ATP

(B) Pyruvate

(C) ADP

(D) Glucose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The electron transport chain is a series of four protein complexes that couple redox reactions, creating an electrochemical gradient that leads to the creation of ATP in a complete system named oxidative phosphorylation.

Concept note-2: -The process of forming ATP from the electron transport chain is known as oxidative phosphorylation. Electrons carried by NADH + H+ and FADH2 are transferred to oxygen via a series of electron carriers, and ATPs are formed. Three ATPs are formed from each NADH + H+, and two ATPs are formed for each FADH2 in eukaryotes.

Concept note-3: -The end products of the electron transport chain are water and ATP. A number of intermediate compounds of the citric acid cycle can be diverted into the anabolism of other biochemical molecules, such as nonessential amino acids, sugars, and lipids.

Concept note-4: -The electron transport chain is located on the inner membrane of the mitochondria. The electron transport cycle yields around 30-32 ATP molecules, according to current studies.

Concept note-5: -The products of ATP breakdown are adenosine diphosphate (ADP) and a phosphate ion. Adenosine diphosphate and the phosphate ion can be reconstituted to form ATP, much like a battery can be recharged.
