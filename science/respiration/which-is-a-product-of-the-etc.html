Which is a product of the ETC?

(A) Carbon dioxide

(B) Glucose

(C) Oxygen

(D) ** Water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The end products of the ETC or electron transport chain are the formation of ATP, NAD+, FAD, water, and protons. ETC works as a proton pump since it pumps hydrogen ions or protons through the membrane, and only allows ATP synthase for the formation of ATP.

Concept note-2: -The electrons flow through the electron transport chain, causing protons to be pumped from the matrix to the intermembrane space. Eventually, the electrons are passed to oxygen, which combines with protons to form water.

Concept note-3: -ScienceBiologyThe importance of water forming in the Electron Transport Chain is so electrons continue to move as oxygen is accepting electrons to form water ATP can be stored for longer periods of time.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: When a pair of electrons enters the electron transport chain is results in the production of one molecule of water. This means that each NADH that enters the electron transport chain one molecule of water is produced.
