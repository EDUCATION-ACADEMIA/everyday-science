How many valence electrons does Phosphorus have?

(A) 31

(B) ** 5

(C) 15

(D) 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Therefore, the valence electron in a Phosphorus atom is 5.

Concept note-2: -The highest-numbered shell is the third shell, which has 2 electrons in the 3s subshell and 3 electrons in the 3p subshell. That gives a total of 5 electrons, so neutral phosphorus atoms have 5 valence electrons.

Concept note-3: -Elements having 5 valence electrons are placed in Group 15. The elements of group 15 are nitrogen, phosphorus, arsenic and antimony.

Concept note-4: -Phosphorus is having 5 electrons in the valence shell. If it is written in orbital pattern (in higher level studies) it will be written as 2s2, 2p3. If phosphorus is using only electrons from p then the valence will be 3. If both the electrons from “s” as well as “p” are used the valence will be five.

Concept note-5: -Therefore, the element in the fourth period of the periodic table which has five valence electrons is Arsenic. Note: The atomic radius, ionisation energy, and electronegativity of elements in the same period appear to follow a pattern.
