What is a balanced equation?

(A) when you cannot create or lose mass in a reaction.

(B) the ratio of actual yield to predicted yield times 100

(C) ** an equation with an equal number of atoms in the reactants and products

(D) what happens when two substances react to produce entirely new substances.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A balanced equation is one in which the number of atoms of each element is the same on the side of the reactants and on the side of the products. Q. The number of atoms of the reactants and the products of various elements on both sides are equal in a balanced chemical equation.

Concept note-2: -A balanced equation is an equation for a chemical reaction in which the number of atoms for each element in the reaction and the total charge is the same for both the reactants and the products. In other words, the mass and the charge are balanced on both sides of the reaction.

Concept note-3: -A balanced chemical equation is one which contains an equal number of atoms of each element on both the sides of the equation.

Concept note-4: -Hence, If the number of atoms of each element on both sides of a chemical equation is equal, then the equation is called a balanced chemical equation.

Concept note-5: -The equation in which the number of atoms of each element is equal on both sides (Reactant and Product side) of the equation is known as a balanced chemical equation.
