Periodic events are used for the measurement of time.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Within a reasonable error of measurement, sunrise and sunset are periodic events. Also new moon occurs periodically. Hence, these events can be used to measure time.

Concept note-2: -To measure time, the event should be such that it repeats after an equal interval of time. Such a type of motion is known as periodic motion.

Concept note-3: -Solution : The motion of a pendulum is a periodic event which can be used to measure time or the duration between two events.

Concept note-4: -Events that take place after regular interval of time are called periodic event. Example: rising of sun every morning is a periodic event.

Concept note-5: -In an experiment to determine the acceleration due to gravity g, the formula used for the time period of a periodic motion is T=2√7(R−r)5g. The values of R and r are measured to be (60±1)mm and (10±1)mm, respectively. In five successive measurements, the time period is found to be 0.52s, 0.56s, 0.57s, 0.54s and 0.59s.
