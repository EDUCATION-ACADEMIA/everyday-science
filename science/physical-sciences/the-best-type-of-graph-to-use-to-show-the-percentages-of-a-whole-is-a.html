The best type of graph to use to show the percentages of a whole is a

(A) bar graph

(B) ** circle graph

(C) line graph

(D) scatter plot

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A pie chart, sometimes called a circle chart, is a way of summarizing a set of nominal data or displaying the different values of a given variable (e.g. percentage distribution). This type of chart is a circle divided into a series of segments. Each segment represents a particular category.

Concept note-2: -Pie charts are used to show parts of a whole. A pie chart represents numbers in percentages, and the total sum of all the divided segments equals 100 percent.

Concept note-3: -Pie charts can be used to show percentages of a whole, and represents percentages at a set point in time. Unlike bar graphs and line graphs, pie charts do not show changes over time. The following pages describe the different parts of a pie chart.

Concept note-4: -A circle graph, or a pie chart, is used to visualize information and data. A circle graph is usually used to easily show the results of an investigation in a proportional manner. The arcs of a circle graph are proportional to how many percent of population gave a certain answer.

Concept note-5: -Circle graphs are most useful when comparing parts of a whole or total. Bar graphs also make comparisons easily. Unlike most circle graphs, bar graphs compare exact amounts. Circle graphs are used when dealing with percentages, and the percentages of the pieces add up to 100 percent.
