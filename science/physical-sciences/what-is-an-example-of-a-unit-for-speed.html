What is an example of a unit for speed?

(A) m/s

(B) mph

(C) km/hr

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -We measure distance in the units of metre (m) or kilometre (km). We measure time in the units of second (s) or hours (h). Hence, the units for measuring the speed are m/s or km/h.

Concept note-2: -Speed is a measure of how fast something is travelling. The most common units of speed are metres per second (m/s), kilometres per hour (km/h) and miles per hour (mph).

Concept note-3: -unit of speed is meter per second. This means that to cover a distance of 1 meter, the time required is 1 second.

Concept note-4: -kilometres per hour (symbol km/h); miles per hour (symbol mi/h or mph); knots (nautical miles per hour, symbol kn or kt); feet per second (symbol fps or ft/s);

Concept note-5: -Hence, the SI Unit of speed is metre per second or m/s or m.s-1.
