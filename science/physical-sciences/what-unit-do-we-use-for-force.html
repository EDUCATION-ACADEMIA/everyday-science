What unit do we use for force?

(A) ** Newton

(B) grams

(C) kilograms

(D) pounds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The SI unit of force is the newton, symbol N. The base units relevant to force are: The metre, unit of length-symbol m. The kilogram, unit of mass-symbol kg.

Concept note-2: -The newton (symbol: N) is the SI unit of force. It is named after Sir Isaac Newton because of his work on classical mechanics. A newton is how much force is required to make a mass of one kilogram accelerate at a rate of one metre per second squared.

Concept note-3: -In the international system of units (SI unit) Kilogram (kg) is a unit measuring the mass of an object. Newton is a derived SI unit used to measure force.

Concept note-4: -One newton is the force needed to accelerate one kilogram of mass at the rate of one metre per second squared. 1 N=1 kgm/s2.
