The amount of space an object takes up

(A) Weight

(B) ** Volume

(C) Mass

(D) Matter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Volume simply refers to the space an object takes up. Depending on the physical state of an object, there are a couple ways to measure volume. If we are trying to measure the volume of a box, for instance, we would multiply the length of the box by its height and by its width.

Concept note-2: -The space occupied by an object is called its volume. The SI unit of volume is cubic meter.

Concept note-3: -Volume (V) is the measure of the amount of space taken up by an object. Volume is measured in cubic centimeters, which are also called milliliters. To find the volume of a rectangular object, you can multiply its length by its width by its height. This gives you the total amount of space that the object fills up.

Concept note-5: -Answer: Mass is the amount of mass an object contains. Mass is measured using a scale. Volume is the amount of space matter takes up.
