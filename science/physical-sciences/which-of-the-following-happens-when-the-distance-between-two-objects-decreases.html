Which of the following happens when the distance between two objects decreases?

(A) ** The objects’ gravitational pull increases

(B) The objects’ gravitational pull decreases

(C) The objects repel one another

(D) The contact force is greater

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, when the distance between the objects is reduced to half, the gravitational force increases four times.

Concept note-2: -The force of gravity depends directly upon the masses of the two objects, and inversely on the square of the distance between them. This means that the force of gravity increases with mass, but decreases with increasing distance between objects.

Concept note-3: -Increasing the separation distance between objects decreases the force of attraction or repulsion between the objects. And decreasing the separation distance between objects increases the force of attraction or repulsion between the objects.

Concept note-4: -Answer. Answer: The force of gravitation between two objects is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them therefore the gravity will become four times if distance between them is reduced to half.

Concept note-5: -Hence, according to the question, if the distance between the objects increases, then the gravitation force between them decreases.
