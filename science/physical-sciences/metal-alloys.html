metal alloys

(A) ** homogeneous mixture

(B) heterogeneous mixture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Alloys are homogeneous mixtures of two or more elements. The elements could be two metals, or a metal and a non-metal.

Concept note-2: -An alloy is considered as a homogenous mixture because its composition remains uniform.

Concept note-3: -Alloys are made when two or more metals are mixed together. They usually are homogeneous mixtures. Examples include brass, bronze, steel, and sterling silver.

Concept note-4: -An alloy is a homogeneous mixture of two or more metals or a metal and non-metal.

Concept note-5: -An alloy is a mixture of two or more metals, or a metal and a nonmetal, in which the composition of each element used is uniform throughout the volume. Brass(copper and zinc), bronze(copper and tin) and steel(iron and carbon) are a few examples of alloys.
