Which term is an example of force?

(A) ** weight

(B) energy

(C) work

(D) mass

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Weight is another word for the force of gravity.

Concept note-2: -The unit of measurement for weight is that of force, which in the International System of Units (SI) is the newton. For example, an object with a mass of one kilogram has a weight of about 9.8 newtons on the surface of the Earth, and about one-sixth as much on the Moon.

Concept note-3: -The weight of an object is defined as the force of gravity on the object and may be calculated as the mass times the acceleration of gravity, w = mg. Since the weight is a force, its SI unit is the newton.

Concept note-4: -There are many examples of forces in our everyday lives: weight force (i.e. the weight of something) the force of a bat on the ball. the force of the hair brush on hair when it is being brushed. the force of your foot pushing on the pedal when you ride your bike.

Concept note-5: -Force is simply defined as the push or pull movement. Different types of force are contact forces and non-forces. Some examples of force are Nuclear force, gravitational force, Frictional force, magnetic force, electrostatic force, spring force and so on.
