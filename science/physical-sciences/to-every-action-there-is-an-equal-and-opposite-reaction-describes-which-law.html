“To every action there is an equal and opposite reaction” describes which law?

(A) Law of conservation of momentum

(B) Newton’s 1st Law

(C) Newton’s 2nd Law

(D) ** Newton’s 3rd Law

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Newton’s third law of dynamics states that every force implies an equal and opposite reaction force.

Concept note-2: -Newton’s Third Law: Action & Reaction His third law states that for every action (force) in nature there is an equal and opposite reaction. If object A exerts a force on object B, object B also exerts an equal and opposite force on object A. In other words, forces result from interactions.

Concept note-3: -Newton’s third law states that when two bodies interact, they apply forces to one another that are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. The third law is also known as the law of action and reaction.

Concept note-4: -Other examples of Newton’s third law are easy to find. As a teacher paces in front of a whiteboard, he exerts a force backward on the floor. The floor exerts a reaction force in the forward direction on the teacher that causes him to accelerate forward.

Concept note-5: -Learn about Newton’s third law of motion, which states that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
