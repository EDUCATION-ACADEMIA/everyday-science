An ice cube melts because:

(A) The ice freezes solid.

(B) The ice dissolves in water

(C) ** Heat is being added and the particles begin to vibrate

(D) The particles bounce freely

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When you heat ice, the individual molecules gain kinetic energy, but until the temperature reaches the melting point, they donâ€™t have energy to break the bonds that hold them in a crystal structure. They vibrate more quickly within their confines as you add heat, and the temperature of the ice goes up.

Concept note-2: -No particles are destroyed and none are added. In addition, the size and shape of the particles does not change. Therefore matter, and mass (or weight), is conserved (stays the same) during phase change, contrary to what many students think.

Concept note-3: -As energy is transferred to the water molecules in the ice, the motion of the molecules increases. The motion of the molecules increases enough that it overcomes the attractions the water molecules have for each other causing the ice to melt.

Concept note-4: -Ice melts when heat energy causes the molecules to move faster, breaking the hydrogen bonds between molecules to form liquid water.

Concept note-5: -The number, size and shape of individual particles stay the same when ice melts. For both ice and water, the warmer the temperature, the more the particles move.
