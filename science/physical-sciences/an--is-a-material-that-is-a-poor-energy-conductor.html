A(n) ____ is a material that is a poor energy conductor.

(A) Copper Wire

(B) ** Insulator

(C) Metal pan

(D) Electric Circuit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An insulator is a term given to materials which are bad conductors of electricity.

Concept note-2: -Insulators or bad conductors are materials that do not allow electricity to easily pass through them. Atoms in such material have tightly bound electrons, which prevent the flow of electric current. Examples of bad conductors include-Wood, Rubber, Glass, Sand, etc.

Concept note-3: -Carbon, nitrogen and bromine are non-metals which are poor conductor of electricity.

Concept note-4: -Materials that are poor conductors of electricity are called insulators. Wood, glass, plastics, and non-metal elements made of hydrocarbon polymers are among the examples. As electricity cannot pass through these materials.

Concept note-5: -Lead is an example of a poor conductor. It is a poor heat conductor because it quickly forms lead oxide by interacting with the environment, and metal oxides are poor heat conductors.
