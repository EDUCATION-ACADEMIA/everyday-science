Which one of the following reptiles is toothless?

(A) Crocodile

(B) Chameleon

(C) Alligator

(D) ** Turtle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reptiles are turtles, snakes, lizards, alligators and crocodiles.

Concept note-2: -Few exceptions do exist such as turtles and tortoises, who are the only toothless reptiles. Instead, they have sharp beaks that help them eat a variety of herbivorous, omnivorous, and carnivorous diets.

Concept note-3: -But unlike cows, turtles don’t have any teeth at all! Like a bird, they have a beak. With their strong jaw muscles and hard beaks, carnivorous turtles crush the shells of their prey – crabs, sea urchins and clams, for example. Baby turtles have something called an egg-tooth, or caruncle, but it’s not really a tooth.

Concept note-4: -They include crocodiles, snakes, lizards, turtles, and tor-toises.
