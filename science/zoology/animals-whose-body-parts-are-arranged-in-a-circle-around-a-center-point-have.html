Animals whose body parts are arranged in a circle around a center point have

(A) ** radial symmetry

(B) spherical symmetry

(C) asymmetry

(D) bilateral symmetry

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Radial symmetry is found in the cnidarians (including jellyfish, sea anemones, and coral) and echinoderms (such as sea urchins, brittle stars, and sea stars).

Concept note-2: -The most common animal body plan is radial symmetry. Animals with radial symmetry have body parts organized around a central point (notice on the flower where the dotted lines come together), like the spokes in a bicycle wheel.

Concept note-3: -For example, pies, snowflakes, and starfish are radially symmetric because they have many different lines of symmetry (dividing them into matching halves) and the lines cross one another at the center.

Concept note-4: -Radial symmetry is the arrangement of body parts around a central axis, as is seen in a drinking glass or pie. It results in animals having top and bottom surfaces but no left and right sides, or front or back.
