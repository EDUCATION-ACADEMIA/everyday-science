Worms that contain male and female sex organs are called:

(A) Males

(B) Females

(C) Asexual

(D) ** Hermaphrodites

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Earthworms are hermaphrodites, meaning an individual worm has both male and female reproductive organs. Earthworm mating typically occurs after it has rained and the ground is wet. They emerge from the soil and jut out their anterior end.

Concept note-2: -True hermaphroditism is a rare form of intersex anomaly where both male and female gonads are present. True hermaphrodites diagnosed in the past have been preferentially reared as males.

Concept note-3: -hermaphroditism, the condition of having both male and female reproductive organs.

Concept note-4: -An organism possessing both male and female organs is called hermaphrodite. Males in sexually reproducing species have organs that create male gametes, often called sperm. Female gametes, referred to as eggs, are produced by various sexual organs in females.
