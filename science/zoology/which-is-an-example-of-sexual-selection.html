Which is an example of sexual selection.

(A) ** Female peacocks choose the males with the longest tails even though the tails make it harder for males to survive.

(B) Female deer choose the fastest bucks to mate with because they are well adapted to the environment.

(C) A female fig tree swiped left on Tinder.

(D) Female hornets have a better chance of surviing in an environment than males.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Male peacocks, known for their brightly colored jewel-toned feathers, show off their colorful train or tail to attract a female. When it comes to selecting a mate, peahens (female peacocks) decide what is attractive. Peahens often choose males for the quality of their trains.

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is A. Peahens choose to mate with peacocks that have the most beautiful tails. Sexual selection arises from differences in mating success when there is a preference by one sex for certain characteristics in individuals of the other sex.

Concept note-3: -The degree of sexual dimorphism found in the majority of species of birds-of-paradise (cryptically coloured females while males have spectacular ornamented plumages and elaborate courtship displays) is one of the most prominent avian examples of sexual selection.

Concept note-4: -Female choice refers to the situation where female members of the species choose certain males based on their displays or traits. One such example is the bird-of-paradise, where males have colorful plumage and will perform mating dances.
