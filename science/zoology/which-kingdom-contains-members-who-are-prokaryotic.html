Which kingdom contains members who are prokaryotic?

(A) ** Archaebacteria and Eubacteria

(B) Archaebacteria and Protista

(C) Eubacteria and Animalia

(D) Eubacteria and Fungi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Complete answer: Kingdom Monera is classified into two domains namely archaebacteria and eubacteria. Kingdom Monera contains the least organized unicellular prokaryotic microorganisms on earth. Both archaebacteria and eubacteria are unicellular microorganisms, which are usually called prokaryotes.

Concept note-2: -The kingdom Monera consists of unicellular, prokaryotic, microscopic, and the most ancient organisms. The kingdom Monera is divided into two kingdoms namely, archaebacteria and eubacteria.

Concept note-3: -Under the five-kingdom classification given by R.H. Whittaker, prokaryotic organisms are placed in the kingdom Monera.

Concept note-4: -All the living organisms having prokaryotic cell structure are classified under Kingdom Monera. Thus they are microscopic, prokaryotic, heterotrophic organisms. Most common example is bacteria.

Concept note-5: -Abstract. The archaebacteria are a group of prokaryotes which seem as distinct from the true bacteria (eubacteria) as they are from eukaryotes.
