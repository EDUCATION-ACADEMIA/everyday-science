Ammonia and methane in the atmosphere combined with oxygen to form ____

(A) Co and CO2

(B) ** co2 and other gases

(C) Hydrogen

(D) .ozone

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Free oxygen combined with methane and ammonia to form water, carbon dioxide and other gases.

Concept note-2: -Changes in the atmosphere The proportion of ammonia decreased as it reacted with the newly formed oxygen in the atmosphere to form nitrogen and water vapour. The proportion of methane decreased as it reacted with the newly formed oxygen to form carbon dioxide and water.

Concept note-3: -Photosynthetic organisms combine CO2 and water (H2O) to produce carbohydrates (such as sugars) and emit oxygen as a by-product. Places such as forests and areas of the ocean that support photosynthetic microbes, therefore, act as massive carbon â€œsinks", removing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere via photosynthesis.

Concept note-4: -Methane is the main component of natural gas. Methane enters the atmosphere and eventually combines with oxygen (oxidizes) to form more CO2. Methane converts to CO2 by this simple chemical reaction.
