Which of these is an example of a bivalve?

(A) snail

(B) ** clam

(C) earthworm

(D) octopus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bivalve mollusks (e.g., clams, oysters, mussels, scallops) have an external covering that is a two-part hinged shell that contains a soft-bodied invertebrate.

Concept note-2: -Oysters, clams, mussels, scallops, cockles, coquinas, and many more are all part of the class Bivalvia. Most soft-bodied, aquatic animals with two shells fall into this group. Bivalves are found in all types of water.

Concept note-4: -The class includes the clams, oysters, cockles, mussels, scallops, and numerous other families that live in saltwater, as well as a number of families that live in freshwater. The majority are filter feeders.

Concept note-5: -Clams and their relatives (oysters, scallops, and mussels) are often called bivalves (or bivalved mollusks) because their shell is composed of two parts called valves. Bivalves have a long history. Their fossils first appear in rocks that date to the middle of the Cambrian Period, about 510 million years ago.
