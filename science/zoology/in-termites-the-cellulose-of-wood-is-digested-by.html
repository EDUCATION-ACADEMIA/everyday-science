[SOURCES]
In termites, the cellulose of wood is digested by.

(A) ** Protozoa

(B) Bacteria

(C) Protozoans and bacteria

(D) Cellulose breaking enzyme

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The termite gut teams with protozoa which is essential for the secretion of the enzyme. Microbes in the gut of a termite break down cellulose into easily digested sugars and short-chain fatty acids. These fatty acids are taken into the cells of the termite and used as nourishment.

Concept note-2: -Symbiotic cellulolytic bacteria from the gut of the subterranean termite Psammotermes hypostoma Desneux and their role in cellulose digestion.

Concept note-3: -Mixotricha protozoans digest cellulose for termite metabolism.

Concept note-4: -Termites have a symbiotic relationship with microorganisms called protozoa that live in the insect’s gut (figure 4.1. 4. a-a). The termite benefits from the ability of the protozoa to digest cellulose, a carbohydrate important in plant structure.

Concept note-5: -"Termites live on cellulose, mostly from the dead wood they chew, but they depend on protozoa in their gut to provide the enzymes that can digest the wood, ” Poinar said. “These protozoa would die outside of the termite, and the termite would starve if it didn’t have the protozoa to aid in digestion.
