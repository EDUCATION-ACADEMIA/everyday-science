Structures on clams that allow water and other materials to enter and leave are called

(A) ** siphons

(B) gills

(C) radulas

(D) trochopores

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A siphon is one of the canal structures used for irrigating farmlands. It is an irrigation facility used for conveying irrigation water that passes waterways such as creeks, ditches, rivers or in any form of the bodies of water.

Concept note-2: -A siphon is a tube that allows liquid to travel upward, above the surface of the origin reservoir, then downwards to a lower level without using a pump. When a certain amount of water moves over the bend in the siphon, gravity pulls it down on the longer leg lowers the atmospheric pressure in the bend of the siphon.
