What type of symmetry is organized around a central point?

(A) ** radial

(B) assymetry

(C) bilateral

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In radial symmetry the body has the general form of a short or long cylinder or bowl, with a central axis from which the body parts radiate or along which they are arranged in regular fashion.

Concept note-2: -Radial symmetry is the arrangement of body parts around a central axis, like rays on a sun or pieces in a pie. Radially symmetrical animals have top and bottom surfaces, but no left and right sides, or front and back.

Concept note-3: -radial symmetry. noun. : plant and animal symmetry in which similar parts are arranged in a balanced way around the center of the body compare bilateral symmetry.

Concept note-4: -Radial symmetry is found in the cnidarians (including jellyfish, sea anemones, and coral) and echinoderms (such as sea urchins, brittle stars, and sea stars).
