Characteristics of Phylum Arthopoda:

(A) ** segmented, coelomate, bilateral symmetry, exoskeleton, jointed appendages

(B) true segmentation, coelomate, parapodium

(C) endoskeleton, unbranced appendages, no segmentation, acoelomate

(D) 3 pair of legs, 3 body segments, exoskeleton, trachae

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They possess an exoskeleton. They have jointed appendages. Their body is segmented. They are bilaterally symmetrical. They possess an open circulatory system.

Concept note-2: -Organisms belonging to the phylum Arthropoda have characteristic jointed appendages. Other features include sensory organs and statocyst.

Concept note-3: -Arthropods are covered with a tough, resilient integument or exoskeleton of chitin. Generally the exoskeleton will have thickened areas in which the chitin is reinforced or stiffened by materials such as minerals or hardened proteins.

Concept note-4: -The phylum Arthropoda contains a wide diversity of animals with hard exoskeletons and jointed appendages. Many familiar species belong to the phylum Arthropoda-insects, spiders, scorpions, centipedes, and millipedes on land; crabs, crayfish, shrimp, lobsters, and barnacles in water (Fig. 3.72).

Concept note-5: -Exoskeleton. An exoskeleton is the supporting structure on the outside of the body of an arthropod. Segmented bodies. Jointed appendages such as mouthparts and antennae. Bilateral symmetry. Dorsal blood vessel. Ventral nerve cord.
