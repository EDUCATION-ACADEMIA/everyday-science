Herpetology

(A) study of mammals

(B) ** study of reptiles

(C) study of worms

(D) study of life

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is the discipline of zoology concerned with the study of amphibians (includes frogs, toads, salamanders and caecilians) and of reptiles (includes snakes, lizards, amphisbaenids, turtles, tortoises, crocodilians, and tuataras). Herpetology is concerned with poikilothermic, or ectothermic, or cold blooded tetrapods.

Concept note-2: -Reptiles are known to have common ancestors with birds but birds are studied under a specialized field of study called Ornithology. Also, the field of study dedicated to the study of only amphibians is known as Batrachology and the field dedicated to the study of snakes is called Serpentology (or Ophiology).

Concept note-3: -variants or less commonly ophidology. ˌäfəˈdä-, ˌōf-plural-es. : a branch of herpetology concerned with the study of snakes.

Concept note-4: -batrachology (uncountable) (zoology) The study of amphibians.

Concept note-5: -What does the role of a herpetologist include? “It’s about studying and exploring the secret lives of reptiles and amphibians that may include frogs, toads, salamanders, newts, snakes, turtles, terrapins, crocodiles, alligators, and lizards.
