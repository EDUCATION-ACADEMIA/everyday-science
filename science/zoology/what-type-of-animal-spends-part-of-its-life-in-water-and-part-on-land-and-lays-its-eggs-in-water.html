What type of animal spends part of its life in water and part on land and lays its eggs in water?

(A) ** amphibians

(B) reptiles

(C) birds

(D) fish

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amphibians: The animals which can live both in water and on land are called Amphibians. Frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts are among the species that make up this category. Before amphibians reach adulthood, they first experience a larval stage, where they breathe primarily through their gills.

Concept note-2: -Amphibians! Amphibians are a group of vertebrates that has adapted to live in both water and on land. Amphibian larvae are born and live in water, and they breathe using gills.

Concept note-3: -All amphibians spend part of their lives in water and part on land, which is how they earned their name-“amphibian” comes from a Greek word meaning “double life.” These animals are born with gills, and while some outgrow them as they transform into adults, others retain them for their entire lives.

Concept note-4: -Amphibians can live on both land and water. Amphibians like to live near water bodies. Amphibians can breathe on land as well as in water. Frogs, salamanders and toads are some examples of amphibians.

Concept note-5: -Amphibians are small vertebrates that need water, or a moist environment, to survive. The species in this group include frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts. All can breathe and absorb water through their very thin skin. Amphibians also have special skin glands that produce useful proteins.
