A comparison between a bird’s wing and a bat’s wing is a classic example of ____ structure.

(A) Mutated

(B) Vestigial

(C) Homologous

(D) ** Analogous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Analogous structures are anatomically different structures though they perform similar functions. The wings of birds, bats, and insects are analogous structures; they serve the same function of flight but their structure and origin are different from each other.

Concept note-2: -Bird and Bat wings are analogous because they evolved independently. But the wing bones are homologous because they were inherited from the common ancestor.

Concept note-3: -Bird and bat wings are called analogous because they have separate evolutionary Origins but they both perform a similar function which is a key role in flight. Analogous organs are the result of convergent evolution.

Concept note-4: -Such features that overlap both morphologically (in form) and genetically are referred to as homologous structures; they stem from developmental similarities that are based on evolution. For example, the bones in the wings of bats and birds have homologous structures.

Concept note-5: -A great example of analogous structures are a bat’s wing and a bee’s wing. Bats and bees do not share common ancestry, so the structures cannot be homologous. Both bat wings and bee wings serve a common purpose-helping bats and bees fly!
