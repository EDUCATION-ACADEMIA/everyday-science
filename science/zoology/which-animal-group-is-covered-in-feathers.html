Which animal group is covered in feathers?

(A) Amphibians

(B) ** Birds

(C) Fish

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, the correct answer is ‘Birds’. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-2: -Birds are the only animals that have feathers. Feathers provide a light but tough covering, and keep birds warm in cold conditions. They also help the birds to fly. The outer feathers around a bird’s body form a smooth, streamlined surface, helping the bird to cut through air as it flies.

Concept note-3: -Feathers are epidermal growths that form a distinctive outer covering, or plumage, on both avian (bird) and some non-avian dinosaurs and other archosaurs.

Concept note-4: -Birds are the only living animals to have feathers. The only other animals known to have these colorful appendages were their ancestors: the non-avian dinosaurs.

Concept note-5: -Wing feathers. Tail feathers. Contour feathers. Semiplume. Down. Filoplume. Bristle.
