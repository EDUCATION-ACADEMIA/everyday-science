What is the largest known amphibian?

(A) Giant Indonesian Frog

(B) ** Japanese Giant Salamander

(C) The Giant Bullfrog

(D) The Goliath Frog

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Japanese giant salamander can grow to a length of 5 feet (1.5 m) and a weight of 55 pounds (25 kg). The largest wild specimen on record weighed 58 lb (26.3 kg) and was 4.46 ft (136 cm) long.

Concept note-2: -The Chinese giant salamander is the world’s largest amphibian, reaching lengths of more than 1.8m. They have been revered in Chinese culture for thousands of years, but overexploitation for the luxury food market as well as habitat loss has devastated wild populations.

Concept note-3: -World’s largest amphibian identified as a unique species. A critically endangered Chinese giant salamander, Andrias davidianus, at Atlanta Zoo. There are at least three species of Chinese giant salamanders, new research shows.

Concept note-4: -Japanese giant salamanders are the second-largest salamander species in the world. These long-lived amphibians lurk in Japan’s rocky, fast-flowing streams.
