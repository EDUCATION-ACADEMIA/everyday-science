Which type of asexual reproduction involves the unequal division of an organism causing an outgrowth which may eventually fall off and form into a new organism?

(A) ** Budding

(B) Gemmules

(C) Fragmentation

(D) Parthenogenesis

(E) Fission with regeneration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Budding. Budding is a form of asexual reproduction that results from the outgrowth of a part of a cell or body region leading to a separation from the original organism into two individuals. Budding occurs commonly in some invertebrate animals such as corals and hydras.

Concept note-2: -BUDDING. A type of asexual reproduction in which a new organism develops as an outgrowth of the parent is called budding. The new organism, called a bud, is a tiny duplicate of the parent organism. In budding, the nucleus divides equally and the cytoplasm divides unequally.

Concept note-3: -Budding is an asexual reproduction method in which a new organism develops from a bud of an existing organism. Until the new organism matures, it remains attached to the parent organism. Hydra and yeast, for example, reproduce by budding.

Concept note-4: -Fragmentation. Fragmentation is another mode of asexual reproduction exhibited by organisms such as spirogyra, planaria etc. The parent body divides into several fragments and each fragment develops into a new organism.

Concept note-5: -Fragmentation is a form of asexual reproduction where a new organism grows from a fragment of the parent. Each fragment develops into a mature, fully grown individual. Fragmentation is seen in many organisms.
