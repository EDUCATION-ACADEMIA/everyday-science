In mammals,fertilization occurs in.

(A) Ovary

(B) ** Fallopian tube

(C) Uterus

(D) Vagina

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The oviduct or Fallopian tube is the anatomical region where every new life begins in mammalian species. After a long journey, the spermatozoa meet the oocyte in the specific site of the oviduct named ampulla, and fertilization takes place.

Concept note-2: -In mammals, fertilization takes place internally in the protected environment of the ampulla of the oviduct, as opposed to external fertilization where sperm and egg meet outside the parent’s body (e.g., as in fish, reptiles, amphibians and invertebrates).

Concept note-3: -A pregnancy starts with fertilization, when a woman’s egg joins with a man’s sperm. Fertilization usually takes place in a fallopian tube that links an ovary to the uterus. If the fertilized egg successfully travels down the fallopian tube and implants in the uterus, an embryo starts growing.

Concept note-4: -In rabbits, humans and other placental mammals, internal fertilisation takes place inside the fallopian tube.

Concept note-5: -Internal Fertilization This occurs in most bony fish, many reptiles, some cartilaginous fish, most amphibians, two mammals, and all birds. Reptiles and insects produce leathery eggs, while birds and turtles produce eggs with high concentrations of calcium carbonate in the shell, making them hard.
